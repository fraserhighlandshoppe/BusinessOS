# FHS Marketing Plan

## Overview
Marketing plan supporting FHS Sales Objectives and Business Strategy.

## Core Principles
**Marketing is the KEY money-maker** - an ongoing process, not a one-time activity.

**Key Insights**:
- Start with understanding customer needs/wants
- Customers hate to be sold, love to buy
- Marketing triggers ACTION
- Success proportional to perceived value created
- Everything is a test - run marketing like a business
- Find customers where they ARE

## Our Brand

### Brand Representation
- Logo, website, store, vehicles, staff
- Should tell customers what we sell and what kind of business we are

### Brand Definition
1. Decide what product/service to highlight
2. Describe company personality (luxury, budget, fun, reliable)
3. Identify best customers (age, gender, interests, economic status)
4. Define unique selling proposition

## Our Message
Develop message that:
- Resonates with customers
- Motivates them to buy
- Is lean and focused

## Strategic Objectives
Support [FHS Sales Objectives](FHS_Sales_Objectives.md)

### SWOT Alignment
- Capitalize on strengths
- Improve weaknesses
- Maximize opportunities
- Minimize threats

### SMART Goals
1. **Website as Community Tool**
   - New blog content weekly (happenings, product news, new products)
   - Add video monthly
   - Customer case studies (blogs, videos)
   - Expert interviews (blogs, videos, audio)
   - Standing pages for product lines (1 new/page per month)
   - Expand keyword coverage

2. **Customer Database Growth**
   - 25% annual increase (2% monthly)
   - Newsletter with online sign-up
   - Every in-person/phone contact = data entry
   - Highland Games sales = customer info
   - Draws requiring contact info = newsletter signups

3. **Referral Program**
   - Program rules drafted by end of March
   - Advertise existence:
     - Website by end of April 1st week
     - Emails by end of April
   - Advertise benefits to referrers
   - Coupons for new referees
   - Permission to publish happy customers (end of summer)

## Marketing Mission
Educate pipers, drummers, and dancers about our existence. Show products we carry and why we carry them. Set proper expectations for complete customer satisfaction.

## Marketing Channels

### Traditional
- Classified advertising (now online)
- Direct mail (postal code targeting)
- Networking groups
- Public relations (go-to person for industry info)
- Trade shows (consider costs before exhibiting)

### Digital
- Website as community education tool
- Social media (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
- Email newsletter
- Content marketing (blogs, videos, case studies)
