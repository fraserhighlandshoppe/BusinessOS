Capital Accounts are not static. They can change daily. The accounts are balanced and closed at the end of the
accounting period.
Profits cause the account to increase. Losses cause the account to decrease. Losses are indicated with brackets.

| 10,000 Net Income
| 60,000 New Balance

Smith, Capital
--------------| 50,000 Initial
| (10,000) Net Income
| 40,000 New Balance

Another way a persons Capital Account may increase is if the partner invests additional money into the
company. Except for limitations as specified in the partnership agreement, the investment can be at any time.
using drawing accounts
A partner withdrawing assets (cash) from the business will have his capital account reduced, and this is
recorded in the drawing account.
Another time that the drawing account could be recorded against is if the partner has a service contribution
(salary - unincorporated business can't pay salary so the withdrawal comes out of owner capital).

Division of Partnership Earnings
The division of profits and losses are specified in the partnership agreement. If the agreement fails to specify the
division, the law requires that profits and losses are split equally.
Other benefits to specifying the division:
the share of earnings is specified
the share of assets if the business is terminated is specified
it is easier to calculate your share of income for income tax reporting
Division of Net Income or Net Loss
Assets - Liabilities = Total Owners Equity.
Profit is when the Total Owners Equity is positive.

They start the year with a zero balance.
Revenue - inflow of assets from business operations. Have a credit balance.
Expense - outflow of assets from operations. Have a debit balance.
Withdrawals (drawing) - where the owner removes cash for personal reasons/use. Have a debit balance.
The Revenue, Expense and Withdrawal accounts are transferred to the Income Summary account. This allows
the Revenue, Expense and Withdrawal accounts to be closed. The Income Summary Account is another
temporary account allowing the profits or losses to be transferred to the owners capital account.
1. close temporary accounts from the income statement with a credit balance and transfer the balances to the
Income Summary account
2. close temporary accounts from the income statement with a debit balance and transfer the balances to the
Income Summary account
3. close the Income Summary account into each partners capital account
4. close the withdrawals account into each partners capital account.
The net profit or loss is transferred to each partner using the Statement of Changes in Partners Equity report.
Statement of Changes in Partners Equity
Shows any changes in the partners equity and includes the allocation of income from the Income Summary
account. Also included are:
capital balance at the beginning of the accounting period
any additional investments made by the partners
each partners equity earned this period
any withdrawals made by the partner
ending capital balance

Accounting for Changes in a Partnership
A partnership is based upon a contract between specific people. If something happens to cause the partnership
to change, the old partnership ceases to exist. Causes include
addition of a partner
death or withdrawal of a partner
liquidation of the partnership.
These cause the termination of the partnership, but may cause the formation of a new partnership. The books of
the old partnership needs to be closed, and the assets and liabilities transferred to each of the partners.
addition of a partner
If the new partner is injecting assets into the business, their capital account will show the injection, and the cash
or other assets will also show a corresponding increase. The business assets increase.

When adding a partner, bonuses may be paid to either the old partner(s) or new partner(s). A bonus would be

J has 52000 in equity
T has 26000 in equity
M is coming in with 42000 investment, and will have a 25% interest.
52+26+42 = 120000. 25% of 120 is 30,000

A new partner can be given a bonus when their equity contribution is less than their partnership share. You'd do
this when the new partner has exceptional skills.
death or withdrawal of a partner
A withdrawal can happen by the partner selling their interest in the partnership. The cash is outside the business
and only the capital account changes.
A second method s when cash and assets are given by the remaining partner(s) to the withdrawing partner in
exchange for the interest. THis has special accounting procedures and it creates a new partnership.
you may pay no bonus
you might pay a bonus to remaining partner(s).
if you are in a hurry to get out
you might pay a bonus to the withdrawing partner(s).
recorded equity may be understated
departing partner receives assets with a greater value than his share of the equity
The death of a partner immediately dissolves a partnership. The person's estate is then entitled to their equity.
1. all financial statements are prepared

You may want to ensure that the partnership agreement states that the expenses will be deducted from the
revenue before earnings are allocated otherwise the estate could go against the entire revenue.
liquidation of the partnership
1. sell any non cash assets
2. allocate gains or losses
3. pay any liabilities
4. distribute remaining cash to the partners.
