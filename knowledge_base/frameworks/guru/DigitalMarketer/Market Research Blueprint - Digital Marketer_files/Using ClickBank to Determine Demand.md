Using ClickBank to Determine Demand
Just as you can use Ebay for product and pricing research, you can also do the same thing with Clickbank. To do this, let’s check out the ClickBank Marketplace as shown by the site CBEngine. You can also look on ClickBank itself or other CB intelligence sites, but we like CBEngine the best.
Go to CBEngine.com and you should see something like the image below:
Note on the lower right side there’s a search box. This is where you will enter your primary keywords, again for us in our example, we’ll type in “memory improvement,” hit the “Search” button and see results similar to the image below:
You can click on any of these results to get more detailed information about each of the products. Let’s click on “The Ultimate Memory Course” product and see what comes up:
In the image above number 1 provides a product summary. This can be good for helping you determine your product description copy. If it’s a particularly hot selling product, then you may want to borrow ideas from this copy in creating your own.
Number 2 shows a shot of the actual product website, and you can click on the “Visit Website” text by number 3 to visit the site. It’s worth visiting the sites that come up in your research to see if they have any kind of “affiliate” training or information areas. Many do, and you can usually find all kinds of great information for suggested ads, copy, images and other information there.
Number 4 lets you click on several different tab to find out information about payouts, etc. Click and review the information provided by each of these tabs to see if there is anything helpful.
Number 5 shows that CBEngine provides Pro (meaning paid) members information about refund rates and product prices. You can get this information without being a paying member simply by signing up for a free trial. Sign up, get the info you need and then cancel.
Number 6 shows you how many affiliates are promoting the product. The more people promoting the more successful the product. Number 7 gives you the average commission payouts to affiliates and average initial sale and total commissions per sale.
You can use this money to reverse engineer what the sales price and average sale are simply by dividing the average $ per sale by the average commission per sale. In this case divide $11.99 by .5 and you know that the products are selling on average for $23.98. Coolness!
Go through this process for your primary keywords and make notes on your ClickBank Product & Pricing Research Worksheet. Make notes about the products that might be right for each of the steps in your 5 Things Funnel, as well as information about affiliates, payouts, commissions and pricing.
