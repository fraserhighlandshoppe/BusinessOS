Using AdWords Keyword Planner
If you don’t already have an AdWords account, just hop on over to http://Adwords.com and sign up for an account.
It’s free and it will be a valuable tool for you as you move forward to hone your 5 Things Funnel. If you already have an AdWords account, login and click on the “Tools” text at the top of the page as shown in the image below:
When you do that, you will see a dropdown box similar to the one shown in the image below:
Click on the “Keyword Planner” and you will be taken to a screen that looks similar to the one in the following image:
Click on the “Search for new keyword and ad group ideas” field and you will see something like the image shown below:
Now, you’re ready to do research using this tool.
Type the URL of your site or your biggest competitor’s site into the “Your landing page” field and click the blue “Get Ideas” button below. Google will then analyze the page and give you all sorts of ideas that you can use to help you flesh out your 5 Things Funnel.
You can enter in your site or your competitors and definitely run this analysis for as many of your major competitors as you feel comfortable running as the more you do the more you will likely see trends and get better ideas for your lead magnet, tripwire, core offer, profit maximizer and return path.
When you click the “Get Ideas” button, you will be taken to a page that looks something like the image below:
The first thing to check here is whether average monthly searches are trending up, trending down or staying about the same. You can also see whether your market is seasonal or cyclical within any given 12 month period.
In the image above, the average search volume is fairly consistent with mild spikes going into the Christmas/New Year holidays and some fall off in demand shortly thereafter. So, the first test is just to look at the visual data here and see if there are any warning signs. For the memory project, no immediate problems appear.
Next, let’s look lower down in the report, as seen in the image below:
Now, here’s where things start to get really interesting.
The numbers in red circles will help us break down this report to understand all that it offers. Number 1 just shows that we’re in the results page for memory-improvement-tips.com.
Click the arrow at the top of the column with the number 2 so that it sorts in descending order the average monthly search volume. That way, you’ll be able to make decisions based on the largest volume of monthly searches for the terms we’re looking at.
Number 3 shows you the ad groups by relevance according to Google’s users’ searches. This is fantastic data, because now you can get ideas for your funnel based on actual user and prospective buyer behavior.
Note that number 3 presents several interesting possibilities. 22,430 people per month search for “memory game” which continues to bolster our earlier research about this market’s strong interest in gaming. A memory game could make a great lead magnet, tripwire or core offer, and that’s what the letters next to that term mean.
21,700 people per month are searching for “brain” games. This could be a good upsell (DM calls this a Profit Maximizer, this the PM letters beside the keyword) and if we don’t already have a brain game or brain related product to sell, we could just go become an affiliate for someone who does, sell that and take a commission, or we could form a joint venture (JV) with someone or buy private label rights and sell that product as an upsell.
17,090 people are searching for how to “improve memory.” Keep that in mind as a benefit for your product in your copy or ad targeting.
13,330 people are searching for “memory vitamins” or “memory supplements.” I hadn’t thought of this before as I was thinking more of creating a memory course, but of course people would rather just take a pill and remember than have to go through the time and trouble of completing a course. This is definitely something to consider as a lead magnet with a free memory supplement giveaway, or as a $1 tripwire sample or even a core product or profit maximizer.
Another 14,500 people are searching for “Brain Exercise” or “Free Games” and these would make wonderful lead magnets or tripwires.
Also of interest, 5,000 people per month are concerned enough about memory loss to search for “memory loss,” so that could be a good fear based copy angle for our offer.
Do you see how this data is helping to share our 5 Things Funnel?
Repeat this process with the Google Adwords Keyword Planner for your own site (if you have one) and for each of your major competitors. As you do, make notes on the Google Adwords Keyword Planner Worksheet as you see good potential items for Lead Magnets, Tripwires, Core Offers, Profit Maximizers, Copy Benefits/Fear Items and Joint Venture and Affiliate Ideas.
