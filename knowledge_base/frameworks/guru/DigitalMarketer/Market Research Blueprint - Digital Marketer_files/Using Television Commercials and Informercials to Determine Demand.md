Using Television Commercials and Informercials to Determine Demand
Despite all of the ballyhoo about online and digital marketing, almost 98% of the population still watches television. And billions and billions of dollars of products are sold via TV every single year, so doesn’t it make sense that we should look at what’s selling there and see if we can get any additional help with our product and pricing?
Of course it does! So, let’s check out the go-to resource for television marketers, the Jordan-Whitney Greensheet. You can find it at JWGreenSheet. When you go there, you should see something like the following image:
What we’re interested in here is the “rankings” information, particularly for Infomercials and Short Form commercials. Click on Infomercials and let’s check out what’s selling best:
Fitness, cleaning, kitchen accessories and some beauty items are selling best. So, there’s nothing that really helps us here, but if you were selling any of those kinds of products, it would make sense to check out the infomercials about those products and learn from their copy, offer and pricing models.
Also, it would be a good idea to order the products and go through the order cycle taking notes all the way.
Infomercial marketers are some of the best and constantly split-test to maximize revenue from their marketing funnels. If you find someone with a successful infomercial in your niche, definitely research them and learn from them.
You can also check out the Media Analytics report (free with registration from IMSReport.com) as shown below to see if that provides any additional helpful information:
Do this also for each of the Short Form category on the JWGreensheet site, and then record any notes on your Infomercial & Commercial Research Worksheet.
