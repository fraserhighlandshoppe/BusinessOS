Using FreshKey, Merchant Words, and TeraPeak to Determine Demand
That about does it for the free research tools.
If you want to drill down even further, you can use paid tools to provide you with even more in-depth product research information. Chances are though, that you’ve probably got enough now to move on without the need for additional research, so this section is for over-achievers. J
Tools like *FreshKey, Merchant Words and Terapeak are relatively self-explanatory, so we’ll just do a cursory review here.
*FreshKey – *FreshKey is an Adobe Air software product that sells for under $50. With *FreshKey, you can research keywords on Amazon, Ebay, Etsy and other sites. Check it out below:
Number 1 is where you enter your primary keywords to search. Select the site you want to research by clicking the appropriate selection in number 2. In this case, we’re interested in Amazon and Ebay, so we will run separate searches for each of these sites.
After selecting the site you want to research click the “Get Keywords” button shown at number 3, and you will see the top keywords on the site you’ve selected to research.
Use this information to conduct more in-depth research similar to what was described in this section to help you determine the best things to sell in each phase of your 5 Things Funnel and how to price them.
Merchant Words is an Amazon research tool.
The image below shows you the kind of data you can access using Merchant Words.
Merchant words ranges in price from $30 to $900 per month depending on the kind of plan you want.
TeraPeak is another paid research tool. Searching TeraPeak for “memory improvement” yields the following information:
Terapeak is one of the most helpful paid product research tools. Look at what we learned from our search. Supplements are clearly the winner for memory improvement related sales on Ebay. Prices range from about $18 to $35 and with upsells, total revenue ranges from about $14 to $70 on the top best-selling products.
This can give you an idea of what to sell and at what price and, as we always like to see, is based completely on data provided by actual customer purchases.
*FreshKey is no longer supported as of Nov. 2017
