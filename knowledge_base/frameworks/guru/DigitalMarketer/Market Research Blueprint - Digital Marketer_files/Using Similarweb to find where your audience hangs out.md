Using SimilarWeb to Find Out Where Your Audience is Visiting
First, let’s find out where your audience hangs out.
We’ll do that using SimilarWeb.
Pull out your Organic Search Competitor Worksheet, Paid Search Competitor Worksheet and SEMRush Competitor Worksheets. See, all this stuff is incremental and it all ties together.
Now, just type each competitor name into the search box as shown in the image below…
Click the “Search” button and you should see something like the following:
Using the numbers inside the red circles in the image above: 1. Shows you that these are the results for the competitor site you entered into the search box. In our case, I entered http://memory-improvement-tips.com. 2. Shows us that we are in the “overview” display mode. 3. Gives us an estimate of the monthly visits to the site. This is a good very broad frame of reference but is frequently not completely accurate. 4. Gives us an estimate of the engagement level for the site by showing time on site. And, last but definitely not least, 5. Shows us “Referrals” or inbound and outbound traffic.
To see the results, you have to click the greyed out button.
When you do, you should see something like the following:
The first column with the red circle numbered 1. Shows you all of the sites that people went to just before coming to this site. The second column with the red circle numbered 2. Shows you all of the sites that people went to immediately after leaving the site.
This information is extremely valuable as it can tell you a lot about your audience. It can also reveal additional competitors you didn’t know about or compatible interests that you can use for targeting your ads.
Best of all, it can show you places to advertise to get more visitors to your site, and it might even reveal ideas for lead magnets, tripwires, core offers and profit maximizers for your own products and services.
Let’s take a look at our memory-improvemen-tips.com inbound traffic and see what we can learn. Looking at these referring sites, we find that items 1, 4, 5, 9 & 10 are all browser hijacking virus sites. That isn’t very helpful, unless it might mean (and I’m not saying it does) that there is some funny business going on with the site traffic.
A few of the other referring sites have typing and similar games or links on them, which may mean that our inbound traffic folks enjoy playing games. There’s also a bookmarking site, Symbaloo, in the 6th spot for traffic referrals, so that’s worth taking a look at as well.
Inbound traffic isn’t really much help here, so let’s take a look at outbound traffic. The first site, Pogo.com is a gaming site, so that confirms what we found from some of the inbound traffic sites, that our memory improving visitors seem to like games, especially word and word typing games.
The second outbound site is exitmonetization.com. When you Google that, you learn that it’s a way to send exiting traffic to affiliate offers, but it also doesn’t seem like the friendliest thing to do with your visitors. It does give you a new idea and possible option for monetizing your site though.
Yoarcade is another gaming site, further confirming our potential audience’s interest in games, and Miniclip and ArmorGames are also gaming sites.
So, what did we learn from this exercise?
Our audience is definitely interested in games, so on my Inbound/Outbound Traffic Intelligence Worksheet I’m definitely making notes about games for possibilities for several of the 5 Things.
Make notes on your own Inbound/Outbound Traffic Intelligence Worksheet about what you learned about your target audience from analyzing inbound and outbound traffic using the free version of SimilarWeb.com.
Write down any ideas you got from looking at the traffic to and from your competitors’ sites. You can use a different sheet for each competitor URL or just use one sheet and write all your ideas down in one place. The important thing is that you are beginning to let data about actual customer behavior shape your funnel.
