Worthwhile Test Element: Call To Action
The rest of this section will cover some high impact elements you can test on your landing page. These are just elements that I’ve noticed are worth testing. Make sure to evaluate your landing page and pick the elements that you think will help improve your page based on your qualitative and quantitative research.
The call to action is one of the most important parts of your landing page. It needs to stand out and have an actionable message. The first thing you should check is that people actually see your call to action. If they don’t know where to click, they won’t click!
I added an arrow to this image to make sure you could see just how many clicks this button got in comparison to everything else on the page. If your CTA isn’t getting these kinds of clicks, you need to make it more apparent.
This is the same landing page, just with a different view. Notice just how many clicks the CTA got when compared to other clickable areas!
If your call to action stands out, then the next thing to test is the actual call to action copy itself. Make sure it is action oriented and descriptive. Short and obscure CTAs won't likely get the job done.
In this case, the team tried to improve an already descriptive call to action. Notice how a small tweak on this CTA saw such an impressive lift. When you make small changes on important elements, you are bound to see some conversion shifts.
When you have drilled in on your CTA copy and know that it stands out on the page you could try some other aesthetic tests, but I wouldn’t recommend it. These tests might not be worth your time, the amount of lift you’ll see after perfecting your copy won’t likely be worth the time it takes to set up the test.
A more advanced approach would involve using a learning algorithm, such as a multiarmed bandit algorithm to change the button color and game out a few percentage points.
If you are getting to this point on a single element, it’s time to move on and try out some other things to test on your page!
