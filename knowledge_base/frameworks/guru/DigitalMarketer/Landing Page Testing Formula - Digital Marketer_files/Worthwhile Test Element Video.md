Worthwhile Test Element: Video
If a picture is worth a thousand words -- a video has got to be worth like…10,000 words!
Here are just a few tests you can run that might help skyrocket some of your conversions:
Video Length
Similar to copy length, video length is directly connected to the price and complexity of your offer. Longer more descriptive videos are not appropriate for lead generation or small ticket items.
Keep this chart in mind when you are thinking about your video length!
Voice Actor
The accent and gender of your narrator will have an impact on conversions. If you have the resources to get a professional voice actor to narrate your video, make sure to get multiple accents and test the accents regionally.
If you are running a true accent test, keep the script, video length, and all other video factors constant.
CTA Delay
Sometimes you want your visitor to actually listen to what you’re saying and not distract them with your call to action right away. If you are going to try out CTA delays, make sure that your video gives audio cues to the prospect.
Adding in ‘Click that button below’ and having it appear at that moment is a tactic we use here at DM and it has worked well for us.
No call to action is displayed (image left) until the sales arguments have been made --- at which point the buy button is "popped" (image right.)
Remember, just because this tactic has worked well for us doesn’t mean it will for you. Your visitors might be impatient and want to click through right away. Test this!
Autoplay Vs. Click To Play
This is another great test that will likely be impacted by your audience. Corporate B2B audiences tend to bounce on autoplay videos, but a consumer audience will actually see a conversion boost. This is an easy test to set up with your video player, and very worthwhile.
Pro tip: When you create videos --- keep the raw footage! The original video will be needed when you tweak things like length and timing.
