Starting a New Project
At the stop of the spreadsheet, in cell A1, enter the date of your script. Each time you update the script or create a new version, update the date too. This will help you find the most recent version, no matter how many edits or versions have been made throughout the project.
Next to that, in cell B1, type the project name.
Work through the template a step at a time. Write your script in Column B. Add production notes in Column C. Then time each step and put the Time Code in Column A.
Use or adapt the example copy as needed. In the template, you'll find chunks of sales copy from imaginary VLSs for 2 products: a software called the Spymaster 5000, and a diet book called the Chocolate Cake Diet. You'll also see examples we've pulled from real VSLs we've made in the past.
These chunks of script show you the type of messaging you need in each step of the template. If you like the copy, use it. If you need to change it or adapt it, feel free to do that.
Use or adapt the ideas we share in the production notes. Feel free to adapt any ideas or notes we share. You need to clearly spell out what you want to happen in each section of the video. The notes we give you in the template are merely guides.
