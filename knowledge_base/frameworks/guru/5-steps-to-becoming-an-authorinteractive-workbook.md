2

Clarify Your Message. I know, I know... I see
it all the time. You’ve got so much knowledge
and information in that head of yours that
you want to get it all out.

Truth is, you probably have enough content
to fill an entire series of books. But all of your content
should center around one core message. So, keep

4

Craft Your Working Titles. Once you have a
title you can start telling others about your
book with confidence! A catchy title will help
you stand out and will communicate what
your book is about.

Your title can be clever and cute as long as your subtitle explains it further. For example, my client Brenda
Hornung named her book Love Life Again. That’s a

5

Create Your Table of Contents. Here’s the
truth about books. People want short.
You’re busy, right? Your friends are busy...
your clients are busy...who has time to read
anymore?

I suggest you write 8-10 chapters, 8-10 pages each, so
your final book is between 100-150 pages. So decide
on 8-10 main areas you want to cover in your book,
then develop working chapter titles that will serve

8

as the blueprint to guide you in writing your content.
Add in 3-5 subtitles for each chapter so you make sure
you don’t forget anything!
Having an outline will keep your creative juices flowing. As you’re writing your book, pay attention to
other books that are similar. What front pages will you
include, and what next steps will be your call to action
for your reader?
