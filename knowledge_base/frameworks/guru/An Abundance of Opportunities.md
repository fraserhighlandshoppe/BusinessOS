Let’s start off with your names and a brief overview of the business.
Jason: My name is Jason Hrsak, and this is my wife Angela. Our business is Smart Investment
Solutions. It actually started off as just a real estate investment company, but it’s begun to morph
into much more. As our context grows and as we spend more time with the Entrepreneurs Training
Program, we’ve realized that it’s more about finding all cash flowing investments. These can be
small businesses, real estate opportunities, or anything else that can be an asset to cash flow for
people to invest in. Predominantly though, our business remains grounded in real estate.
Our website is www.smartinvestmentsolutions.ca.

How did you get your start in real estate?
Both Angela and I have always wanted to be
entrepreneurs; we’ve always wanted to own
our own business together. And over the past
25 years, we’ve both had day jobs that we
hoped to use as a jumpstart into the world of
entrepreneurship. Unfortunately, that fell through about five years ago, and we were forced to
re-evaluate our plan and begin looking more closely at other options.
Our long term vision was still to get out of the “rat race” and create financial freedom, so we knew
that we had to make a change. I had been following Darren at Fast Track for about six years, but I
had the old mentality of “The teacher’s always there, and when the student is ready, the teacher will
reveal themselves”. In other words, I was attending many of Darren’s seminars, without really
taking action with the content I was learning.
About 18 months ago now, we were at one of Darren’s Super Conferences, and I finally embraced
the fact that I was going to have to make an internal change if I was going to get where I wanted to
be and achieve financial freedom. And real estate seemed like the right way to go!
When we were at the Rich Dad Advisors session in May 2013 and Darren was offering what was
then known as the Coaching and Mentoring Program (now called the Entrepreneur Training
Program), it felt like the right fit for where we were at the time. We knew that we needed to make a
change, we just didn’t know how to go about making it. Knowing Darren’s team and the fact that
they had extensive knowledge and a track record to back it, we joined the training program!

So you’ve only been involved in real estate for the last 18 months?
We’ve been investing in real estate for the last twelve months, but we began learning the ins and
outs through the coaching program as of last April.

What types of properties do you focus on?
Well when we started, the majority of the properties that we invested in were single-family homes.
We’ve done a lot of work with foreign workers in both rural and urban settings, and with businesses
who employ these workers. Many of these businesses have expressed the fact that there isn’t much
housing available for foreign workers in their communities.
We had real estate ideas, thoughts and aspirations, and these employers that we got to know had a
housing need for their workers that they were bringing into the country, so the two went hand in
hand.
From there, it’s grown into more than just meeting the needs of foreign workers and other
businesses. It’s about finding the right real estate on the right side of the S curve, regardless of
whose needs we’re meeting. There are many people who need clean, affordable housing, so there
was a big market for us to tap into to find cash flowing properties and help other investors.
So, although it started as a lot of single-family homes, we quickly realized that the month to month
cash flow needed to replace our day jobs’ just wasn’t there. We got to about five doors and
discovered that single-family homes, despite being a great asset that can create long term cash
flow down the road, wouldn’t be able to fully support us in the short term. We weren’t able to
replace our job income with the cash flow from our new business.
Because of this, we began to look in two different directions. The first was multi-family dwellings.
Currently, we’re working on an eight-door project in Bonnyville; we’re raising capital to pull this
project together. And we also realized from our day jobs that the highest cash flowing asset – by far
– is business. So we want to find something where we can invest in a business, add cash flow and
turn this around into our real estate investments. We haven’t found that business yet; we’re still
sourcing it out. We’ve had a couple opportunities come our way but they didn’t end up being a
proper fit for what we’re looking to accomplish. Hopefully within the coming year we’ll find a
business to invest in that will align with what our competencies are and allow us to expand our real
estate portfolio.

What has been your biggest accomplishment thus far?
One of the models that Angela and I live off is: “Life has no remote control, If you want to change
the channel get up and do it yourself.” So we’ve taken that aspect and applied it to our
entrepreneurial career. We want to see change, so we have to make it happen. Just getting the five
properties within a year is a huge accomplishment for us. In my old context from last year, I would
never have thought it possible. Where I am now though, I feel like it’s not enough. I know we can
do more.
We’re very proud of how far we’ve come, but we also know that there’s so much more out there.
There’s an abundance of available opportunities. It’s just a matter of being able to take your context
and wrap your head around said opportunities. You need to find them and turn them into win-win
scenarios for us, the investor and the client.
Angela, what is your entrepreneur story?
Angela: As Jason mentioned, he’s been following Darren for several years. After events, he’d come
home very excited to tell me about what he’d learned or how his context had changed. Because I
hadn’t participated in any of these events, I didn’t always know exactly where he was coming from.
His excitement was always plain to see, but I didn’t understand how much he was learning and his
vision for our future.
So one day, Jason was at the Fast Track offices talking to Darren,
and Darren said: “I never see your wife at any of the events. Why
doesn’t she come?” And Jason said: “Well, she doesn’t really have
the same context that I do at this time.” Darren pulled out a book,
Rich Woman by Kim Kiyosaki, and said that I should read it.
Jay brought it back and I started reading it, and that’s really when
my context changed. I began to understand more of what he was
trying to do and what his vision was. In addition, I realized that for
myself, coming from a long term family perspective, it wasn’t
necessary for Jason to do this all on his own. I had been almost
sitting back, and that wasn’t the ideal partnership scenario that we
had envisioned. There was no reason why I couldn’t learn and grow
as a person and be a partner in this.
It was also important for me to know that I can grow and be
successful on my own, in the case of anything happening to Jason.
I could run a business, invest in real estate, create positive cash
flow and be financially free. One of the things that I learned from
Kim’s book is that I don’t have to just stand by and wait. There are
many women who take a backseat and just support their husbands and while this support is
important, it’s even better to be involved. There’s no reason why we can’t go out and be successful
together, on our own terms. Integrating the success of our family into the success of our business is
really where it changed for me.
What does working together as a team mean for you?
Jason: The funny thing is that when we started, I was pretty much the driver of the bus so to
speak, but now, over the last year and a half it’s become almost a competition. We’ll look for
properties and I’ll find one, and then she’ll find one, and we’ll compare, and it’s just an extremely
healthy competition where we support each other and grow each other’s strengths.
Angela: And the fact that we’re both on the same page has allowed us to grow our business faster
and has given us a more solidified base. We both have the same vision and the same
understanding, and we’re working together for the greater good in order to get to where we want to
go.
At the end of the day, as a relationship and as a business partnership, it’s important that we both
bring our strengths to the table. There are different things that Jay brings, and there are different
things that I bring. We each contribute in different ways to achieve our success.
What kind of impact has owning your own business had on your family life?
Angela: An absolutely positive impact. It’s strengthened
us; we have two children and I think that what we’ve
learned and what we’re doing is going to rub off on
them. We teach our children about business and about
entrepreneurship, and about going out and working for
themselves. We try to get them involved as much as we
can. Our business and our vision has really strengthened
our relationship with each other and with our children.
Jason: There are so many different things that it has
improved. Take time planning, for example. Obviously
there’s a lot that goes on throughout the day when
you’re supporting two kids, but when you’re involved in
business together, there aren’t any arguments as to who
does what and why. We support each other in both of these aspects of life.
Our kids have even become mini-entrepreneurs. Our daughter Makaila loves going out and looking
at real estate on Sundays. I would even go so far as to say that owning our own business has
created a stronger sense of family amongst us.
Have there been challenges as well?
Jason: For sure!
Angela: It’s always going to be challenging, but at the end of the day we’ve learned that, first of
all, communication is the key. The more we can communicate and share our time plans with each
other, the easier things become.
Jason: [Stuff] happens. You have to look at it and say: “How do I overcome this?” Most people hit
a roadblock, crash into the wall, and fall. We’re always looking for the way to get over or around the
wall. There’s always a way. It’s just a matter of how you look at the problem that’s in front of you.
Angela: You can’t let yourself get frustrated. Either you choose to let things get you down, or you
choose not to let things get you down. And choosing the latter is one of the things that will make
you successful. You are always going to run into problems along the way. It is important to analyze
the situation, learn from your mistakes and keep going!
What kind of advice would you give to other entrepreneurial families?
I would say keep your focus, stay strong, believe in yourself and you can accomplish anything!
It’s all about small steps. Do what you can, when you can. You need to always be pushing yourself;
the more you learn, the more you grow. Opportunities will open themselves up to you and you’ll
find your way. I truly believe that everybody can find success. It is just about taking action.

Jason: I’ll add two things to that. The first is that a lot of people think that Rome was built in a day,
figuratively speaking. We think that we have to build our business up practically overnight and turn
it into a million dollar business, but it’s not about that. It’s the baby steps, it’s what you do day to
day to grow yourself and your business.
You don’t have to quit your day job. One thing we learned is that our unfair advantage is our day
jobs. Use what you do day to day to make yourself successful and push yourself down the road that
you want to go.
And the second piece of advice that I would give is that in most relationships, you always have a
single dreamer, but if you can share the dream and do it together, that will just propel you forward
that much quicker. I think that’s what’s accelerated our progress, in fact.
When you make decisions as a family, that means that there are two people who have to be
involved. And if you only have one person with the drive and the vision, with the other person not
understanding or participating, it’s only going to slow your progress.
Why did you join the ETP program? How has it helped you?
There are definitely a few reasons. First of all, it was more to find direction. We needed to know
how to go about undertaking such a task. We needed to grow our context. I knew Darren’s portfolio,
I knew what he had done in the past, and I’m a firm believer in owning your inner circle. The people
that you surround yourself with are going to dictate your success. We knew that if we didn’t start
surrounding ourselves with people who owned properties or businesses, who had that drive for real
estate, we would never get there ourselves.
So a part of it was definitely so that we could be around like minded people. The other part is that
you’re not playing the game wisely if you’re not following in the footsteps of somebody who’s
already been there and done that. Why make the mistakes that they’ve already made. Learn from
them, receive mentorship from them, and most importantly, use them to help shape your team.
The biggest thing that we have gotten out of the program is
our team. I always thought that when it came to growing a
business or growing real estate – or anything of that nature
really – that you’re on your own. I learned that that’s
actually the furthest thing from the truth. You have a vision
or an idea, and your team brings it together. I have zero
lawyer skills, zero accountant skills, but I know that when
the time comes and I’ve found a deal, I can rely on my real
estate agent. I can rely on my lawyer. I can rely on my
accountant. I can rely on my team. And these people that I
lean on are the ones who drive my business. It’s not so
much about what you know, it’s about who you know.
Angela: I think where a lot of entrepreneurs fail is in trying to do everything themselves. You see
this all the time and it’s impossible, honestly. One thing that we’ve definitely learned from the
coaching program is to avoid this. Find a team, find a group that works for you, find people that you
can rely on and trust. The people on your team are going to be the difference; they’re going to
make you that much more successful and enable you to grow your business. Doing it all by yourself
simply isn’t feasible, it will hold you back.
Jason: A lot of us take the time for personal development, whether this be a Fast Track event or
some kind of motivational speaker, and we always come back fired up, pumped up, ready to take on
the world and make change happen! But people are like water. We follow the path of least
resistance. Everyone ends up back in their day to day routines at their day jobs. With the ETP
program, however, you constantly have that connection point where you’re back in your inner circle,
you’re back in a space of positivity, and you have people to encourage you and push you in the right
direction if you hit a wall. You have a safe place to ask questions, and you’re always getting that
needle of adrenalin to keep you focused, energized, and moving forward.
Angela: We’ve built amazing relationships with these people. We’re there to push each other, we’re
there to support each other, and we’re there to help each other. And these relationships are a key
factor in what’s driven our business so far in only the last twelve months.
One last thing I’d like to add. Earlier, we talked about what it is that makes us successful. We
talked a lot about teams, but one of the other things that we’ve learned over the past year is “Your
network is your net worth.” One thing that we started to do which helped us a lot is a continuity
program. Darren talks about it a lot; we go to a lot of events and meet a lot of people and receive a
lot of business cards, but if we don’t end up doing anything with any of these people, nobody
benefits. When you meet somebody in business and exchange contact cards, it’s important to keep
an open line of communication. Just because you may not be able to help each other today doesn’t
mean that you won’t be a perfect match for another project down the road.
We’ve built up a continuity program and it’s contributed to our success this year. We send out a
newsletter every month to update our network about what we’re doing and different opportunities
on the table. We talk about family, financial education and a little bit about everything in between.
Basically, we’re taking things that we’ve learned along the way and sharing them with others who
may not have had a similar experience yet along their own journey.
This has been a very powerful business tool that has helped us to move forward. We started out
with nobody in our continuity program and now we’re at 125 regular subscribers that are getting
more actively involved in our business in just 5 months.

What’s your vision looking forward?
Our short term vision is learning how to better raise capital so that we can get into some bigger
deals. That’s what we plan to focus on over the next couple of years. What kind of capital can we
raise and what can we do with that money to better our business and drive great returns for our
investors.
And from a long term perspective, it’s about opening ourselves up to all sorts of different business
opportunities so that we can keep growing and expand from being a primary real estate focus into a
broader brand. We want people to be able to come to Smart Investment Solutions for business
needs, for real estate needs, for education needs and for mentorship. It’s kind of similar to Darren’s
model in that we’re looking to have many different facets within our growing brand, rather than just
real estate.
Jason: We actually have a motto for our company that we put on our newsletter:
Angela: “Helping people find positive cash flowing investments while building financial education
along the way.”
Jason: I think that’s the key. One thing that we’ve learned from mentoring with Robert and with
Darren is that, as much as it is about you being successful, it’s also about giving back. The more
that you give, the more you receive. We make a point of hosting monthly cash flow game nights.
Also, our friends see our context change and want to sit down and talk about what we’re doing.
We’ve realized that as much as there’s a business to grow, there are also people to grow as well.
There’s an education to give back to people, and we love doing that. It grows your brand, opens
people’s eyes, and encourages investment. It’s a win-win relationship on both sides.
Another thing that we’re looking at long term is bigger properties. This is something that I really
want to get into. I see us, instead of doing single family homes, owning 100 or 200 unit apartment
complexes that we manage. Whether this comes in the form of building from scratch or taking an
existing building and adding on doors, it doesn’t matter. We want to have the ability to find a large
deal and make it work. For us, for the company, for investors, for tenants, for everybody.
The theme of this issue is “abundance.” What does this mean to you?
Angela: When I think of abundance, I think of it relating to opportunities. There are tons of
opportunities out there. The more that you learn and grow as a person, the more that you expand
your context, the more opportunities you’ll not only be able to see, but also to understand and to
analyze in order to discern what is a good fit for you. There’s an abundance of opportunities out
there for everyone, and it’s just about being able to open yourself up to acknowledge them and take
action.
The other thing that resonates with me when I think of abundance is support. A lot of people are
scared to become entrepreneurs or go into business because they don’t “know it all.” One of the
things that I’ve learned over the past year is that there is an abundance of support out there. There
is an abundance of people who are willing to help you, to teach you, and to share their stories with
you. You don’t have to think that you need to do this on your own. That way leads to failure;
accepting other positive people into your inner circle leads to success.
Jason: I think of abundance like content. I know this sounds redundant, but there’s an abundance
of abundance around. There is an abundance of business opportunities, of family opportunities, and
of relationship opportunities. It’s how you take your context glass, open it up, and fill it with that
abundance that will make you a happier businessperson and a happier person in life within your
relationships. The more you can open yourself up, the more abundance you can take in.
