YOUR MARKET: Session 1
Introduction To “Your Market”
In this session we're going to dive into your MARKET. If you get your market right, your marketing
almost takes care of itself. If you don't get it right, the marketing becomes very diﬃcult. Learn how to
really get inside the mind of your prospect in this session...

• You market is your product and services... and the people who buy them. If you get the market
right, if you understand the people in it and what they want to buy, then the marketing almost
takes care of, creates, and writes itself. And if you don't get the market right, then the marketing is
very diﬃcult... you're always guessing, trying to figure out the various pieces.

• With 80-90% of the business people who are struggling with their marketing, it becomes clear
that they don't really understand their market: who it is that's buying their stuﬀ and what are the
specific outcomes they want. Your market drives your marketing.

• The theme here is "getting inside the head of the customer." And getting so inside your customer
that you say, "As my prospect, my biggest need is... my biggest fear is..." Whenever you're working
on your marketing, speak and think as your prospect.

• When we get our market just right, when we get the language and compassion just right, and the
prospect says "That person gets me. This was made for me. It was created for me, as a unique
individual."

• Specific phrases have high value with your prospect. One phrase can have 10x the persuasion
value as another phrase, even though it means the same thing as a another word you were going
to use. Usually the high value phrase is the word the prospect uses themselves.

• Understanding your market is probably the most important element to "get," and it's he one that
the fewest business owners do get. Most people are interested in sales techniques or lead getting
techniques, instead of understanding the specific market better. Understanding your market
requires eﬀort, energy, insight, doing research, studying, and really trying to challenge yourself to
understand your market better and better. It's hard. And it has a long term payoﬀ. It's not
something that you do once and then you're done. That's not how it works. It's not an exercise of
choosing who you want your market to be, rather it's becoming who your marketing needs and
wants. This requires persistence. You refine it over time. The clearer you get, the clearer your
communication becomes, and the clearer it is to your prospect that you know what you're talking
about and that you're the one for them.

• If you can really get your market, it creates more long term fulfillment as a business person. In
other words, the better of a job your products and services do at meeting your customers needs,
the most security you have internally because the less you fear competition. You're meeting your
customers needs at the ultimate level.

• One of your key roles is to understand your customers' needs better than anyone else... including
your customers. It'll also be necessary to delegate tasks to the other people of your company. You
become the Chief Understanding Oﬃcer, passing on your understanding of the customer on to
the people who work for you. You'll say things like "I'm thinking like my customer right now. And if
he reads this copy, it'll be a disconnect for him. Here's why..."
