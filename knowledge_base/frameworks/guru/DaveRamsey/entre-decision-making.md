DECISION-MAKING CHECKLIST
One of the hardest parts of being a small-business owner is making the tough calls. It’s
easier to simply put it off. Unfortunately, it’s the worst thing you can do. Indecision can
paralyze you and your business.
Action Step: To combat the indecisiveness, use the checklist below. Start by writing down
an upcoming big decision. It can be business or personal. Then, follow the steps. By the
end, you’ll have a clearer picture of the choice you’ll need to make. Before you start, though,
print out a few blank copies, so you can make going through the checklist a habit for any
tough call you have to make.
Decision to be made:

SET A DEADLINE
Battle the “put-off” syndrome by giving yourself a self-imposed deadline and placing it
on your calendar. It will force you to deal with whatever is happening.

TAKE THE PROPER AMOUNT OF TIME
How long should you take to make a decision? It depends on the type of choice
you need to make. For monumental ones, take longer. Small decisions should be
almost instantaneous.

GATHER ALTERNATIVE CHOICES
Options take away a lot of the fear of making the wrong choice. If something doesn’t
work, plan B is always available.

CONSIDER WORST-CASE SCENARIOS
Assessing potential pitfalls and making a plan to address them will set your mind at
ease and help you avoid unrecoverable decisions.

AVOID FINANCIAL RISKS
When making a decision, Dave always asks himself and his team if the company can
handle the financial hit if everything goes south. In fact, when an idea is launched, it
must meet the following financial criteria:
»» If the product fails, the consequences can’t be financially fatal.
»» No money can be borrowed to launch the product.

DECISION-MAKING CHECKLIST
DO YOUR RESEARCH
The more facts and figures you have, the easier it is to recognize the best choice. Like
options, information removes fear.

USE YOUR GUIDING VALUES
When you know the principles that guide you, it’s easier to make any decision. If
anything you are thinking about doing goes against your values, walk away. Processes
can change, principles don’t.

ASK AN EXPERT
Like gathering information, having an expert on your side gives you the power to pull
the trigger. Find a competent one and make the most of their knowledge.

SEEK THE ADVICE OF YOUR SPOUSE
Who better to get an opinion from than the person who loves you the most and
ALWAYS has your best interest at heart? Oftentimes, your spouse will be your
wisest counsel.

WRITE A REPORT
When all else fails, write out a report on the decision that has to be made, including
possible solutions. It’s amazing how many times the answer becomes evident simply by
putting pen to paper.
