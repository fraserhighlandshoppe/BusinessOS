4 FINANCIAL HACKS TO KEEP
YOUR BUSINESS ON TRACK
Is your bookkeeping becoming an uphill battle?
Take the following steps each month to keep on top of it and win the war.
1. Reconcile Your Checkbook
At a minimum, your checkbook needs to be balanced within a couple of days of your
statement arriving. With today’s technology, though, you don’t even need to wait that long. It’s
incredibly easy to reconcile your account every day as transactions clear the bank.
Quick Tip: Cloud-based accounting software is easy to use and super cheap. There are
many highly functional versions available for as little as $15 per month. These programs
eliminate data entry and effectively reconcile your account as you go along.

2. Make Cash Deposits a Daily Habit
Fraud is an enormous problem for small businesses—one in three will experience theft.
Checks and cash should never be left lying around. Set a time each day for someone to
take the deposits to the bank.
Quick Tip: Ask your banker for a check scanner that scans checks and deposits them
electronically so you don’t have to keep going to the bank. Most are free. Many financial
institutions also have apps that allow you to scan from your phone.

3. Close P&Ls by the 7th of the Following Month
All your previous month’s income and expenses need to be posted by the first week of the
month. It gives you time to review the numbers and make corrections and adjustments.
Quick Tip: Set a deadline for the last day of the month for all expenses and receipts to be
given to your accountant or bookkeeper. They will then have enough time to close out the
books. Set tough consequences for those who don’t meet the deadline.

4. Pay Bills at Least Twice a Month
It’s never good business to constantly string your vendors or suppliers along, waiting to
the last minute to pay them. It kills relationships and is just bad business.
Quick Tip: The simplest way of paying your bills on time is to create a budget before you
buy anything. When you allocate every dollar, paying your bills is easy. You’ve already
planned for the expense, and the money is there.
