___

Post your workshop in the local paper as a community event: You can often
advertise for free under “community events” if it’s a free event.

___

Print Fliers or Postcards: Put them everywhere, or enlist your friend or a teenager
to help! Hair salons, wellness centers, yoga studios, doctor’s offices, your church, spiritual
centers, etc.

___

Invite people using Social Networking Sites: Facebook, Twitter & LinkedIn:
Ask local organizations or leaders in your community if you can post the free event on their
wall. Use the event function on Facebook to send people private invitations. Ask friends and
referral partners to post on their Facebook and social media as well.

___

Send out reminder emails 2-3 times to registrants: Have people register, and in
your workshop description or on your flier, let them know there are “limited seats” and to
register early. Always send reminder emails 1, 3 and 5 days before your event.

___

Invite registrants to bring a friend: When you send your reminder emails, invite
registrants to bring a friend, you can even offer a prize for anyone who brings a friend like
a 10% coupon to use on your services, or a free gift like an mp3 or an assessment, or
chocolate!

___

Attend Networking Groups and meet referral partners to help you get the
word out: Find networking groups in your area and seek out the leaders in the group.
Show up in service. Ask what people are up to and what they need next in their business.
Brainstorm how you can support them. Be generous. Then share how you can help them,
ask if they can help you get the word out about your upcoming free workshop or what ideas
they have.

___

___

Invite a guest speaker or sponsor to help you fill the room: Invite other experts
or speakers to provide 10 minutes of content that compliments your topic. Let them know
they can have the speaking slot in exchange for recruiting at least 6 people for the talk. Let
them offer a free gift to attendees as a way to market themselves.
Use Meet-up Groups or speak to existing groups to get the word out:
Launching your own meet-up is a big time investment, but can be very effective… learn
more at meetupzoo.com. A shorter, simpler strategy is to contact existing meet-up groups
and ask the organizer of the group if they accept outside speakers. Then, bring your workshop
material to the existing Meetup and wow them!
