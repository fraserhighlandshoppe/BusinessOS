Assuming a $100,000 critical illness policy payout when a qualifying diagnosis is made and initial drug
expenses of $15,000.
o $1,500 of this expense can be reimbursed back to the owner/employee through the FlexSave
spending account
o The annual deductible on the SSQ plan would be satisfied and $13,500 reimbursed through the in
province catastrophic plan.
o Result: $15,000 is reimbursed to the client. $100,000 in CI proceeds intact.
Next expenses result from a trip to the U.S. for tests at a total cost (including travel) of $6,000.
o The $6,000 in medical treatment and travel cost is not reimbursable through the SSQ plan but
can be claimed through the FlexSave account.
o Result: $0 coming out of the CI plan funds. $100,000 in CI proceeds intact.
Alternative therapy costs of $10,000 incurred but are not an eligible expense on either FlexSave or the
SSQ plan..
o CI funds used for this. Critical illness policy proceeds remaining reduced to $90,000.

Overall effect: $31,000 in expenses are paid for with only $10,000 paid out of pocket with personal dollars
from the CI proceeds. The critical illness policy payout will now last longer.
