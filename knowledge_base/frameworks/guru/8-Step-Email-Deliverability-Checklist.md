INFRASTRUCTURE
This is the foundation for your emails and deliverability strategy. This is what you need to set up and
ensure is set up correctly.
CHOOSING THE RIGHT TYPE OF IP
Should you be using a shared IP or a dedicated IP?
DEDICATED IP
A dedicated IP is used by a single sender source. If you’re a high-volume sender,
you may need multiple dedicated IPs.
SHARED IP
A shared IP is used by multiple sender sources. Use a shared IP if you send under
50,000 emails a month or if you send emails and campaigns infrequently.

SETTING UP THE RIGHT TYPES OF AUTHENTICATION
Have you set up your mandatory authentications? Why should you set up the
optional DMARC?
SPF (SENDER POLICY FRAMEWORK)
SPF veriﬁes the sender address.
DKIM (DOMAINKEYS IDENTIFIED MAIL)
DKIM authenticates that an email was sent from a legitimate and authorized
source.
TLS (TRANSPORT LAYER SECURITY)
TLS encrypts and delivers emails securely.
DMARC (DOMAIN-BASED MESSAGE AUTHENTICATION, REPORTING,
AND CONFORMANCE)
DMARC ensures your authentication is consistent across inbox providers.

SETTING UP FEEDBACK LOOPS
How do you know when a recipient has marked your email as spam? What
can you do about it?
If you set up feedback loops, inbox providers will email you when a
recipient has marked one of your emails as spam. You’ll need to set up
separate feedback loops for all the signiﬁcant inbox providers you send
to.

PROCESSES
These are the recurring activities that you need to maintain. You’ll need to routinely optimize these
processes to ensure smooth data acquisition and management.
AVOIDING SPAM TRAPS
What are the two types of spam traps and how do they aﬀect your
deliverability? What can you do to avoid them?
RECYCLED SPAM TRAPS
Recycled spam traps are abandoned email addresses that have been appropriated by spam trap operators. Sending to recycled spam traps indicates poor list
cleansing practices. (See step 6: Optimizing list cleansing)
PRISTINE SPAM TRAPS
Pristine spam traps are email addresses created speciﬁcally to catch spammers.
Sending to a pristine spam trap indicates poor email acquisition practices. (See
step 5: Optimizing email acquisition)

OPTIMIZING EMAIL ACQUISITION
How can you guarantee new subscribers are legitimate?
AVOID THIRD-PARTY AND POORLY-SOURCED LISTS
Third-party lists have low opens, clicks, and engagements – all negative actions
which will have an adverse eﬀect on deliverability.
RUN BASIC VALIDATION
If your landing page doesn’t have validation, you’ll have to manually look for role
accounts (e.g. sales@domain.com), fake addresses (e.g. alsdkalskd@test.com), and
errors (john@hotmal.com).
USE DOUBLE OPT-IN
Double opt-in requires recipients to conﬁrm this subscriptions after providing
their email address.
QUARANTINE NEW RECIPIENTS
Only add new recipients to your mailing list once you’ve conﬁrmed there’s no
hard bounce.
LIMIT YOUR WELCOME EMAIL VOLUME
Mix your welcome emails with your regular email blasts. A good rule of thumb is
10% welcome emails with 90% regular emails.
SET EXPECTATIONS WITH YOUR WELCOME EMAIL
Clear expectations makes engagement easier down the line!

EMAIL
This is the actual email that gets sent to recipients. There’s the structure of the email, which you’ll need to

blast.
OPTIMIZING EMAIL STRUCTURE
How can you optimize email templates for deliverability?
INCLUDE A LINK TO UNSUBSCRIBE
Recycled spam traps are abandoned email addresses that have been appropriated by spam trap operators. Sending to recycled spam traps indicates poor list
cleansing practices. (See step 6: Optimizing list cleansing)
INCLUDE A LINK TO THE PREFERENCE CENTER
Having a preference center allows you to tailor to the individual subscriber –
boosting engagement and deliverability.
CONFIRM YOUR HTML
Make sure the frontend renders correctly and the backend is bug-free!
INCLUDE A PLAINTEXT AND WEB VERSION OF THE EMAIL
Not all recipients and platforms have enabled HTML – so you’ll need a plaintext
and web version of the email too.

OPTIMIZING EMAIL CONTENT
How can you optimize email content for deliverability?
BALANCE TEXT AND IMAGERY
Focus on communication and readability (i.e. text) with a complementary design
(i.e. images).
CHECK YOUR LINKS
Email ﬁlters will analyze all links in an email. You need to ensure all links go to
reputable domains.
AVOID BASE64
If you don’t know what base64 is, you’re probably not using it. If you are using it
– stop. Spammers use base64 to hide email content from ﬁlters.
