Before We Begin...
Let’s clarify a couple of things! Here are the most
commonly asked questions we get from rookie
coaches…
1. Do I have to have a certiﬁcation or be licensed in
order to call myself a coach?
No, certiﬁcation or licensure is not required to call
yourself a coach. That’s not to say that having additional
credentials from a coaching certiﬁcation program won’t
help add to your credibility, it’s just that they are not
absolutely necessary.
2. Do I have to have an educational background in
business in order to own my own business?
No, speciﬁc or graduate level training in business is not a
prerequisite to becoming an independent business
owner or entrepreneur.

6. Client Service Agreement and Contract
One of the most fundamental pieces of running a
successful and proﬁtable online coaching practice has to do
with the client service agreement and contract you send to
your clients once they have agreed to compensate you
monetarily for the services you provide. Your contract does
not need to be incredibly lengthy nor does it have to be
drawn up by an attorney, at least not at this time. What you
do need to have is simply a Word document that states the
terms and conditions of this service agreement, contains
editable ﬁelds where you can insert dates, names, pricing,
services, and signatures.
The ﬁrst rule of business in any business is to have the client
sign the contract before you begin working with her. The
second rule is to get, at minimum, a deposit or ﬁrst
payment from the client before you deliver the service. We
cannot tell you how many times we made this mistake in
the very beginning of our coaching businesses. Thankfully,
we’ve learned our lessons well and now always institute this
policy in any and every client relationship we have.
We have provided a very basic template for you at the end
of this document. Please make sure that you change all of
the text to reﬂect your business name, your name as well as
your client name, dates, services, and signatures.

The Top 5 Rookie Coaching Mistakes and
How to Avoid Making Them in Your
New Online Coaching Business
Rookie Coaching Mistake #1 – You try to be a “One Size Fits
All” type of coach.
Ex. “I just love helping people and I want to work with
anyone who needs our help.”
Fix – Choose one or two main areas of interest you naturally
do well and enjoy doing. Although it may be scary at ﬁrst to
focus on only a couple of areas for the time being, this will
help you overall to connect with the type of clients you wish
to work with.
Rather than trying to be a one size ﬁts all coach, claiming
your expertise in one or two areas will help you to stand out
in a crowded online marketplace.

Rookie Coaching Mistake #2 – You underestimate yourself
and your ability to help others.
Ex. “I’m not sure I have what it takes to be a successful
coach.”
Fix – Let’s face it, if you do not believe you have what it
takes to be a successful coach neither will anyone else.
Although it can be scary to put yourself out there you’ve got

set of gifts and talents that you can use to help others.
Try writing out several positive afﬁrmations in the present
tense that begin with, “I AM…” and meditate upon them
every single day for as long as it takes for you to believe in
yourself. Journaling and other forms of visual imagery can
also help you to develop the conﬁdence you need to be the
amazing coach you already are. Last but not least, get out
there and starts coaching people. Yes you will have a lot to
learn along the way, but that’s all a part of the journey.

Rookie Coaching Mistake #3 – You agree to coach your
client(s) WITHOUT getting them to sign a Client Service
Agreement and WITHOUT making an initial payment.
Ex. “I trust others and its okay if they don’t sign a
contract or pay me before we start working together.
I’m sure they’ll pay me afterwards.”
Fix – This one is simple. Create a Client Service Agreement
template in Microsoft Word or in Google Docs that includes
all the speciﬁc details of the coaching relationship. Fill in
the correct names, dates, and pricing structure for each
client and save the document as a PDF for yourself before
sending it to your client via email. If you use a paid service
like Adobe Echo Sign, there will be a space for your client to
electronically sign the agreement. If however you do not
have a paid program like Adobe Echo Sign, have the client
print the PDF, sign it, and either mail it, faxed it, or email it
back to you within 48 hours.
You will also want to create an invoice for your client using
your free PayPal business account. Once you create the
invoice in PayPal, you can email it directly to your client.
Once your client has returned the signed document and
made an initial payment, then and only then set up your
ﬁrst coaching session.
