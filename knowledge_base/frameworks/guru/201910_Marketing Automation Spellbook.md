Why Automation?
So many business owners get overwhelmed by the thought of automation. Stories
of machines taking jobs and robots taking over the world litter our social media
feeds everywhere, so it is easy to feel uneasy about the uncertainty..
While many of the stigmas out there are #FakeNews, one thing is certain;
Automation is not going anywhere and is only getting more sophisticated and
useful.
Bottom line…? Get on board, because automation is no longer a luxury. It’s a
necessity. So, what can automation do for you? The short answer… A LOT!
• Frees up your time
• Put your focus in your zone of genius
• Creates consistency
• Delivers quality experiences for your prospects and customers
• Reduces human error
• Improves client and customer retention
And most importantly, continue nurturing your audience from lead to client
without adding more work to your plate.
My favorite definition of marketing automation comes from automation expert
Jermaine Griggs, who simply calls it, “scaling personal attention”. People want
and expect experiences and interactions with brands fit their exact needs and
wants. You can’t do this effectively without automating your efforts, because there
are not ever going to be enough hours in the day.

Marketing Automation Myths
Before we get into how to implement some really incredible, moneygenerating automation strategies, let’s bust some of the myths I often
hear from business owners about marketing automation.

Myth #1
Automation will remove me from my business.
This one couldn’t be any further from the truth! In order to automate
anything in your business, you need to first identify and document
your processes and create systems that can be repeated. And that is
one of the most engaging and hands-on activities you’ll ever do in
your business.
This process of automating doesn’t remove you from the business, but
rather allows you to dive in deep to design intentional and repeatable
experiences for your lead, prospects, and clients.
There are few things more rewarding than architecting an
automation system that can function without someone’s manual time
or ongoing input but really CONNECTS with the people it interacts
with - EVEN for those right-brained creatives.

Marketing Automation Myths

Myth #2
Automation is impersonal.
While on the surface, this sounds true, let’s dig a bit deeper.
Remember the definition of “scaling personal attention”?
Marketing automation allows you to construct a system that will
deliver very relevant information to your prospects based on who they
are and where they are in their customer journey.
For example… when a prospect enters your space, they come with a
problem or pain point and are looking for you to solve it.
You can use automation to funnel them down a marketing path that
is specific to their needs. By doing so, you’re presenting that prospect a
PERSONALIZED solution to their unique problem rather than
trying to convince them of the features and benefits of your product
or service.

Marketing Automation Myths

Myth #3
I need more leads.
Ok… I won’t argue that one needs to have leads in order to run a
profitable business.
But if you’re like most of the businesses we work with, you probably
already have a database of leads.
So it's not a lead problem. It's a conversion problem. The good news is,
converting a warm lead is often less expensive than finding a new
lead.
Marketing automation allows you to create follow up systems that
will PERSONALLY reach out to prospects and customers as they
show interest. You know the saying, “people buy when they’re ready
to buy, not when you’re ready to sell.”
Creating follow up systems allows you to stay top-of-mind by
sending timely and relevant information to your prospects
throughout the buying process - when THEY are ready.

Lead/ Sales Inquiry Follow Up
OVERVIEW
This is easily the most common business strategy to automate. There are dozens of
tools on the market to help you follow up automatically from requests on your
website or other inquiries from the internet or phone, but few businesses leverage
this system effectively.
A study by Harvard Business Review found that prospects are seven times more
likely to buy from you if you respond to their inquiry within one hour.
But, the same study found that 63% of companies take longer than this to
respond to inquiries.
One of the fastest ways to make your investment in marketing automation pay
for itself is to implement a strategic and personalized marketing system for
responding to leads as soon as they contact you.
APPLICATION
This campaign can be triggered by a webform submission, text into a specific
number, social media inquiry, phone call or any other number of ways. It’s easy
to automate and should include a welcome message along with other links to
helpful information.
If you want to maximize your follow up automation system, automate
additional touchpoints after the initial response. Use your best content!

Relationship Building Campaign
(Know, Like, And Trust Campaign)

OVERVIEW
This campaign is similar to the lead/sales inquiry campaign and would likely be
part of the same sequence. The relationship building campaign is designed to
nurture, educate and excite new leads about your brand and products or services.
One of the best ways to do this is to provide relevant and quality information that
your prospects can consume, whether it be a video series, webinar, whitepaper, or
other relevant content.
Use this automated campaign to under-promise and over-deliver.
APPLICATION
As its name suggests, you want your campaign to really build a relationship of
trust, so don’t be salesy or pushy. Ask questions during this campaign to elicit
responses from your prospects so you can gauge their pain points. This will allow
you to tag and personalize your responses.
For example... If you were a chiropractor, it would make sense to discover
(literally) where your prospect’s pain is: back, neck, knees, etc. If you’re a
landscaper, find out if they have dead grass, or not enough time to worry about
their lawn themselves.
Each piece of information you can gather from a prospect will allow you to
strategically market and personalize your campaign for every lead.

Lead Scoring Campaign
OVERVIEW
Lead scoring can help you determine the worthiness or engagement of leads by
attaching values to them based on their behavior relating to their interest in
products or services.
This is generally more useful in businesses that employ sales representatives, but
can certainly be used in other businesses as well.
Note: Lead Scoring is often a feature of an all-in-one marketing automation
system. Not all email marketing systems are capable of easily implementing this
strategy.
APPLICATION
My marketing automation tool ranks prospect engagement and scores a person
based around my desired criteria (such as opening emails, clicking on links,
conversations, videos viewed, etc). I can then see which contacts are more likely to
buy or respond positively to a personal phone call. I can also set it up to
automatically create a task for me to reach out and personally connect.
Basically, when leads reach a specific score, use marketing automation to trigger
important events that only qualified and hot leads would respond to- like coupon
offerings or trigger a notification to the prospect’s sales representative that the
contact is engaging with the business’ content.

Website Visit Automation
OVERVIEW
Setting up and triggering automation around website visitors isn’t anything
new in the digital marketing space. Businesses have been serving up ads based
on your viewing patterns for years (think about the Amazon emails you get
with hyper-targeted product suggestions based on your viewing history).
What is fairly new, however, is the ability for small businesses like yours and
mine to offer this sort of marketing sophistication.
It’s important to think about this automation in two segments. Automation
you own and automation you pay for (advertising).
1) AUTOMATION YOU OWN: Many sophisticated CRMs and
marketing automation tools now allow you to trigger automation when
known contacts visit your site or even specific pages on your site.
2) AUTOMATION YOU PAY FOR: This is most commonly referred to as
remarketing or retargeting. This sort of automation allows you to market
on third party ad platforms, like Facebook and Google, to visitors who have
visited your site or specific pages on your site. This sort of automation is
great for delivering relevant content to warm leads who already know who
you are.

Website Visit Automation
APPLICATION
For the purpose of this guide, I’ll just refer to the automation processes you own. I
recommend setting up triggers when contacts visit pages on your website or other
owned digital properties that show they have buying intent. For example,
someone looking at your pricing or sales page is likely more interested than
someone looking at your homepage or a random blog post. For this reason, I
recommend setting up automation for your “bottom of funnel” content.
You can set up triggers to send engagement emails, texts, or even notify a sales
representative. This could also be useful for simply tracking how long it has been
since a prospect has visited your site.

New Client Welcome Campaign
OVERVIEW
This is one of the easiest wins with marketing automation, but businesses overlook
it so often. Most businesses immediately think about finding new prospects and
customers that they forget about the ones they already have. Setting up a
welcome campaign reaffirms the customer’s decision to buy, and can set proper
expectations for a long-lasting relationship.
APPLICATION
Use this campaign to deliver value and wow your customers in ways your
competitors are not. For example, there are many services out there that will send
handwritten notes on your behalf or send gift cards and brownies without you
having to do anything.
That’s the sort of goodwill that marketing campaigns should be consistently
delivered.

Review & Referral Campaign
OVERVIEW
This one goes hand-in-hand with the New Customer Welcome Campaign and
I’ll almost always build them together. This one, as the name suggests, is very
straight forward, but it’s not just about asking anyone and everyone for a
referral and review.
APPLICATION
To build this campaign effectively, you must first gauge how the customer feels
about the product or service. Was it an amazing experience or did something
negative happen? Using marketing automation and identifying which links
customers are clicking in a very basic survey (usually as simple as an email or
text asking them to click GREAT, OKAY, or NOT GOOD, we can send them
down one of three routes.
If they suggest that their experience was great, then we’ll send them down a
route that asks them for a review, and later a referral or two. If it was anything
other than great, we’ll do our best to make sure we fix whatever the issue is. The
purpose here is to make it as easy as possible for your best customers to give your
business positive reviews.

Automated Webinar & Follow Up
OVERVIEW
Webinars are a great way to capture the full attention of your audience and prove
that you know what you’re talking about. It’s a great way to have them get to know,
like, and trust you while getting them excited about your product or service.

APPLICATION
The best way to tackle automated webinars is, first, to become really good at live
webinars. If you’re doing it right, the live webinars should allow you to find out what
the common objections are and then let you create a great automated webinar covering
all the important aspects and objections.
Automated webinars have the benefit of perfectly tagging (or labeling) prospects based
on their unique situation and then following up accordingly. Normally, I’ll label
prospects into the following buckets:
»» Attended
»» Saw Offer
»» Didn’t See Offer
»» Didn’t Attend
»» Watched Replay
There are even more things you can do, but this should be enough to allow you to follow
up in an individualized way so that each prospect is getting communication, based on
whether or not they attended or saw the offer during the webinar. From there, it’s
pretty straightforward and fairly easy to create automated follow-up campaigns that
are specific to how long the prospect stayed on the webinar or if they completely missed
it.

SMS Opt-ins &
Automated Conversations
OVERVIEW
This is something you’ve likely seen or heard before. You’ll often hear it on the radio as
marketers include a call to action of text [ACTION WORD] to a phone number.
This is a great lead capture tool because it utilizes SMS messaging and mobile phones.
SMS messages have huge open and click-through rates when compared with email, so
going down this route lets you capture and follow up with leads who are more likely to
respond.
NOTE: Not every marketing system can do this on their own… often you need to
connect it to other tech systems to make this one work.

APPLICATION
The example described above is only the beginning of this automated campaign. Using
this feature you can automate replies that get stored in your CRM tool. For example, if
you run a campaign that asks contacts to request information, you can automatically
reply by asking them what their name is. When they reply, you can automatically
store that data in your CRM in the name fields. You can repeat this process for any
number of question & answer combos, including email address, birthday, or other
useful marketing information.
It’s worth mentioning that Facebook Messenger is gaining popularity in this space as
well. There are ‘bots’ that allow you to facilitate this type of two-way, automated
communication using Facebook Messenger, which has become a popular form of
communication.

Long Term Nurture
OVERVIEW
Sometimes a lead or prospect starts moving through your sales processes or
funnels, but stop somewhere along the way before they purchase. In other words,
they just stop progressing toward the end goal of becoming a client. These leads
are generally interested in what you offer, but might not be ready to buy. In this
case, a long term nurture campaign would work well to keep them engaged, even
though they’re not ready to move forward yet.
APPLICATION
During every stage of the customer experience, there’s likely an opportunity for
some of your leads to stop moving forward. Maybe they don’t have enough
interest or are looking at a competitor’s product.
If you think of the customer journey as a freeway, these leads have exited through
an off-ramp and need some love while they’re trying to find their way back to
the freeway.
In a typical business, these leads are considered dead and don’t really get any
attention. In an automated business however, these leads continue to get
occasional messages and follow up prompting them back to the freeway.
The amount and frequency of communication depends on your sales cycle, but the
point is to make sure your leads aren’t forgotten. The purpose of this campaign is
to get them back on the freeway (engaged), not necessarily to buy.

Re-Engagement Campaign
OVERVIEW
This campaign is related to the long-term nurture campaign we discussed above.
If you put someone in a long-term follow-up campaign and they’re really not
interested in hearing from you, then you’re likely doing yourself more harm than
good. A re-engagement campaign is meant to track leads and prospects that
aren’t engaged with your content (they’re not opening or clicking on any emails
as an example), and this campaign provides them a reason to re-engage with you
and your business.
APPLICATION
This is the time to offer your best content or even discounts on popular items to see
if you can pique their interest. If a lead is truly not responding and not
interested, it’s likely better to stop following up with them. You can also use
automation to offer new content and interest through other channels, like social
media or offline methods rather than just email.

Birthday, Anniversary, &
Other Timed Promotions
OVERVIEW
Everyone loves a good birthday, anniversary or other well-timed promotion. If
you collect pertinent date-specific information during the lead collection process,
use that information to stay in touch at relevant times for the prospect.
APPLICATION
This one is very straight forward, but using marketing automation can help
you time it perfectly so that your leads, prospects, and customers get
information on their birthday (or whatever the special date might be).
The difference between a date-specific campaign like this and that of an actual
event is that these dates are specific to the contact and no one else. It’s special to
them, so treat it that way.
The anniversary campaign doesn’t necessarily mean the contact’s anniversary
but could mean their anniversary with doing business with you. I’ve seen real
estate agents use this really well for 1-year special gifts after their client has
closed on their new home (and then ask for a referral of course).
Other applications here include pregnancy due dates, policy expiration dates,
expected graduation dates, etc.

Upsell Campaign
OVERVIEW
As the name suggests, this is all about offering your customers more. As the
saying goes, “the least expensive customer is the one you have.” You likely have
products and services (either your own or affiliate) that complement your core
offering perfectly. Upselling is not about tricking customers to buy more than
what they need, it’s about offering them a complete solution to their problem.
APPLICATION
The automation involved here is really straight forward. Identify who has
purchased your core offer, and if they don’t already have the complementary
product or service, offer it to them.
The timing of this can be crucial. In some instances, it might make sense to do
this during the initial transaction, similar to how McDonald’s offers a combo
of fries and drink with the purchase of a hamburger. Other times it makes
sense to offer an upsell after the product has been consumed and is the next
logical step. Either way, using automation will make sure customers see the
right offer every time and that it’s not forgotten.

Sales Stage Automation
OVERVIEW
Businesses with a sales team know how disjointed things become when
salespeople don’t follow a process. Creating pre-defined stages and automated
systems around those stages help businesses streamline their sales process and
keep all salespeople accountable to the same standard.
APPLICATION
This automation tactic is best used to automate communication and workflow
at different stages of the sales pipeline. For example, you might send an
automated thank you email to the person who referred the prospect or
automatically create a follow-up task for the salesperson when you move the
prospect to a certain stage.
You can also automate communication directly to the prospect as a deal
progresses, letting them know you’re working on different aspects of the
contract or sale (ie, real estate, consulting, law firm, etc). There are a lot of
opportunities here, but the important piece to remember is that as a business
standardizes their sales process, it’s easier to measure and systematically
improve a sales team's performance.

Left Voicemail & Other
Basic Follow Up
OVERVIEW
If you write the same email more than 3 times, you should consider automating it.
Automating basic tasks and workflows like this might not seem worth it at first,
but as you continue to use them, you’ll wonder how you ever got along without
them.
APPLICATION
Chances are you send the same (or very similar) emails and text messages quite
often. Some of the more common ones I’ve seen include the following:
»» I left you a voicemail, please call me back
»» I’ll need you to sign this and get it back to me
»» Here’s a list of our FAQs
»» Here’s that ___ you requested
»» We’ve fixed the issue you called about

Automated Scheduling
OVERVIEW
Scheduling and finding a time to meet with prospects can be a headache.
Matching up calendars and schedules takes time and a lot of back and forth
communication. Using an automated scheduling tool allows prospects to schedule
themselves into your calendar by choosing time slots that aren’t already booked
on your schedule.
APPLICATION
The best way to use this feature is to connect it with automation campaigns to
push people to schedule over a series of follow up emails or text messages and turn
those communications off when they schedule. You can pair this automation
campaign with other tools like video and automated webinars to automate what
you’d normally talk about on the phone with prospects. This makes it easy to
warm prospects up and get them qualified before speaking with them.

Automate Physical Mailers &
Other Offline Communications
OVERVIEW
Incorporating offline tactics in a marketing strategy is less common than it was a
decade ago, but it’s still important. In fact, as digital communication becomes more
mainstream, a well-executed offline marketing strategy will likely become more
important in a rapidly growing digital world.
Sending handwritten cards and letters creates a deeper connection than an email,
SMS, or other forms of digital communication. (I can recall a thank you card I
received from a client several years ago that I proudly displayed on my desk for
several weeks.)
APPLICATION
Using your CRM database and addresses, it’s easy to send physical mailers and
other communications based on triggers your prospects and customers take. For
example, why not trigger a handwritten thank you card or box of brownies to be
sent to your new prospects or customers (without even thinking about it).
This sort of automation separates you from your competitors and encourages
customers to buy more often from you.

Automatically Check In
With a Prospect if a
Quote Has Not Been Viewed
OVERVIEW
People are busy. There’s no arguing that point. Sometimes prospects get busy and
even though they’re interested, they might forget or something gets in the way.
This can easily happen during the quoting process. Using specific tools, you can
automatically resend or follow up with prospects if they haven’t had a chance to
view your quote within a certain timeframe. During the quoting process, time is
of the essence, so making sure prospects see the offer is ultra important.
APPLICATION
The application here extends beyond just viewing a quote. What if a prospect
views a quote but never buys or they stop communicating? This is generally a
good sign they’re not interested, but what if you could automate a coupon code
getting sent to anyone who viewed a quote, but didn’t buy within 48 hours?
These are all opportunities for automating the mundane tasks within your
marketing workflows.

Objections Campaign
OVERVIEW
What can you do if you’ve done your best to persuade a prospect that your offer
is exactly what they need and it’s better than your competitors and they still
don’t buy? I like to create objection campaigns because it lets you collect data and
offer specific marketing to prospects based on their specific objections.
APPLICATION
For example, you can add an email or two at the end of your automated
marketing campaigns that asks a prospect a simple question. It could be as simple
as, “I’ve been emailing you about this amazing offer that you showed interest in,
but you didn’t buy. I’d like to know what we can do to improve this offer for
others. Please click one of the links below and let me know what we can improve”.
From there, you’ll include links like:
»» It costs too much
»» I found a solution somewhere else
»» It didn’t match what I was looking for
»» Something else
As prospects click on the different objections, you can tag (label) them and if you
wish, remarket to them based on their individual objection.
