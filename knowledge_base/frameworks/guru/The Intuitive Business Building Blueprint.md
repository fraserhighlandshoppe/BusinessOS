Introduction
Throughout my career as a corporate ladder climber, psychotherapist, entrepreneur and coach, I’ve
seen so many people fight to rise to the top and become “successful” and along the way they’ve become
unconscious to their deeper needs and out of touch with their natural intuition. This eBook speaks
specifically about how to start shifting your awareness so that you can reconnect with your true essence
of self. As a result, start shifting the way you approach your life so you can become more fulfilled.
I’ll provide you with concepts that will most likely challenge your status quo. Your ability to change your
life as a result of the information I provide, will be a direct correlation to your ability to keep an open
mind, try new things and practice and new way of being.
As with all good books, I’ll start with a story to set the stage. The story is called “This is Water”. It was
part of a commencement speech given by David Foster Wallace at a Kenyon College graduation in 2005.
“There are these two young fish swimming along when they happen to meet an older fish swimming the
other way who nods at them and says, ‘Morning boys, how’s the water?’ The two young fish swim along
for a bit, and eventually one of them looks over at the other and goes, ‘What the hell is water?”
The point of this story is that, more often than not, we can’t see the obvious. We can’t see the “forest
through the trees”. We can’t see the reality that we live in every day because we’re oblivious to it. Are
you conscious of the water you swim in every day? Are you aware of the rip tides, the high and low
tides? I’m going to show you how to navigate the waters you swim in with less stress, fewer obstacles
and a lot more enjoyment. I’m going to show you how to navigate the tides and rough waters with
finesse and confidence. Are you ready to become the Captain of your own ship – the ship of life?

Going Against the Tide – Resistance
As I said in the introduction, you will need to keep an open mind but the reality is … we’re creatures of
habit. We have a tendency to act, behave and think the same way all the time even if we think we are
doing things differently. We are masters of disguise and we may think we are approaching things with
an open mind. However, there will be a big part of you that doesn’t really want to change. A part of you
that doesn’t want to rock the boat. That part of you is your crew member named resistance.
Think about what you read above. I stated that I will be challenging your status quo. I’m certain you had
a physical and emotional reaction to that. It was probably for a split second, and perhaps went
unnoticed, but it happened. Just know, I’m not trying to change who you are, or how you think, feel or
act. I’m simply giving you the navigation charts so you can consider a different path and, perhaps find a
different port of call … maybe a different destination all together.
So, as you feel yourself holding your breath, tensing up, and, most likely, feeling “sea sick” … know this
too shall pass. Simply breathe and ask yourself “what is true and right for me”? As you become more
conscious, more aware, and more intuitive you’ll begin to align more with your truth.
Realize that as you begin to alter your course, and steer your ship in another direction, you’re bound to
go against the tide from time to time. You’ll feel the resistance and, just like the tides, this is completely
natural. With that said, it’s important to know when you’re not just going against the tide and there’s a

You intuition is your guide in all aspects of your life. The issue is that most of us have lost touch with our
intuition. I surmise you’re probably one of them. You were born with intuition but this was quickly shut
down as you learn to navigate life in the physical world.
The reality is intuition has gotten a bad rap. In the past, intuition was considered mystical and
mysterious, seen as “hocus pocus” to most. However, it’s very real and it’s something we were all born
with but have since lost. I do mean we’re ALL born with it because it doesn’t discriminate by gender.
Contrary to popular belief, men are just as intuitive.
In the Western culture, in particular, there isn’t a lot of emphasis on things we can’t see, feel, touch, or
prove. We’re taught to develop a hypothesis then find the evidence to prove that our hypothesis is true.
The evidence may come from science, church, the government, society, media, family or friends. We
look for external resources to prove the hypothesis with the underlying assumption that somebody or
someone or something else knows better than we do. As a result, we develop a sense of distrust in
ourselves.
As the world changes, people are becoming more open minded. As our future unfolds, we’re beginning
to understand that science, physics, logic, and deductive reasoning have their limitations. Just as
Columbus was on his voyage and discovered that the world wasn’t flat after all, we’re learning that
there’s much more to the world around us than we had first thought. As a result, we’re beginning to
explore and, better yet, trust and believe in other methods of gathering information – methods that
don’t rely on hard core evidence. Intuition is one of them!

Discovering Your Inner Compass
One of the reasons you’ve lost your connection to your intuition is because you’re stuck in negative
patterns of perceiving, thinking and behaving. Patterns that, in reality, don’t serve your highest good
and can’t really be trusted. When you rediscover your inner compass you’ll be able to trust what you’re
“picking up” on and make decisions, in life and in business, from a real place.
Have you ever had an experience where you had a “gut feeling”? Perhaps, for some unknown reason,
you felt you needed to take a different way to work one day. Come to find out, there was a huge
accident that happened at that time and you would’ve been in the middle of it. This is your internal
compass, your sixth sense, your gut, your internal navigator giving you guidance as you go through your
day. It’s a feeling rather than a knowing. The more you can tap into this, the more you can trust it and
follow the guidance you’re getting to navigate the waters on your journey.
So, how do you rediscover your inner compass?
To develop this intuition that you have, you need to develop your “in the moment” awareness. Start out
with this exercise:

moment … out loud! It’s best to record this as an audio if you can. If not, just write really fast! Make
yourself comfortable and take a few deep breaths.

What sensation do you feel in your body? What thought crossed your mind? Speak or write constantly
without stopping so that you don’t start to judge or edit what comes up. If you slow down or pause,
your rational mind will get in the way. Don’t worry if you’re not even making any sense.

surroundings, they will all notice different things.
Consider this question carefully … what if you notice what you notice for a reason?
Here is where I may lose you because I will be challenging the status quo. There are no coincidences!
Yes, most people say this, but do they really mean it?! When I say this, I am saying that everything, I

interpreted to mean something. Some of it is conscious and most of it is unconscious. Let’s say you’re
spouse informs you that he/she wants some intimacy tonight but you’re not really in the mood. You

You Don’t Know Why You Know
Let’s start with getting clear on what the word “intuition” even means because it brings about a variety
of different connotations, meaning and interpretations.
Intuition is an illogical, non-empirical way of
getting information in response to a question
and interpreting the meaning.

I say illogical because there really isn’t any rhyme or reason to it. There is no deductive reasoning and
there isn’t a “process” you follow. It’s simply a sense of knowing. It’s non-empirical because it’s not
based on evidence or experience. A good way to explain this is looking at the different ways we know
that a storm is on the horizon. The Captain knows because the radar tells her. Conversely, someone
from a Cherokee Indian tribe can sense that it is coming. The Indian is much more in tune with the
environment and doesn’t necessarily rely on the traditional five senses.
Your intuition comes about in response to questions that are asked. When you ask a question, and
you’re open and aware, the answer will come by what you notice in the world around you.
So, how do you know when you’re being intuitive versus just having a “hunch” or lucky guess? This is
the biggest challenge because, the reality is, you don’t know. It takes practice so that your intuition can
be followed by confirmed experience. As you gain more experience with how intuitive information
“feels” when it comes to you, you’ll learn to make the distinction and trust it.
Before we move on, here are some general indicators to keep in mind.
Intuition comes through images, symbols, and metaphors that need to be translated.
Intuition is objective and perceptive without any emotion or reasoning attached to it.
Intuition is not linear, it’s fragmented without a logical flow.

What Do You Want to Know?
Intuition can tell you anything that you want to know. You start by getting grounded and centered,
asking the question, record your initial impressions then review and interpret them. Really, that’s all
there is to it! A word of caution … you won’t have all the answers because you don’t have all the
questions! Through your intuition you have access to all the answers to all the questions that are asked.
The key is how you ask the questions!
Ever hear the saying, “be careful what you ask for because you might get it”? This is so true! If you want
the answer, you better ask the right question! For instance, if you ask “will it be sunny tomorrow”? The
answer has to be yes! It will definitely be sunny somewhere! So clearly, a properly phrased question is
really important!
Here’s so guidelines on how to ask the question:

Be specific and unambiguous (ie. “will it be sunny in Cleveland tomorrow”?)
Avoid compound questions where there can be two different answers (ie. “Will I get a new job

Make it directly relevant to the issue you want to know about (ie. “Is General Mills a good

“Is General Mills a good short term investment?”)
Avoid being vague (ie. “Should I become a personal coach?” Instead ask “Would I be successful
as a personal coach?”)

Using Your Intuition in Your Personal Life
When you’re using your intuition for personal reasons you need to compartmentalize. What I mean by
this is that you need to push all logical reasoning aside. In fact, you need to separate your knowledge,
judgement, beliefs, emotions, etc. Don’t look to logic, evidence, rational reasoning … simply rely on your
intuition. You’ll keep it separate initially then you’ll integrate it after you get your intuitive information.
Intuitive information is easily lost so it’s important to recognize it quickly. So here’s an exercise you can
use in your personal life where you have all kinds of “other” information such as biases, judgements,
beliefs, opinions, etc.

The Center Technique
Start by asking a question then set it aside.
Now, imagine you’re at the center of a circle, open your mind, and receive an image of yourself. What
do you look like? (Visualize in images, metaphors, etc. not your actual physical description).
Now visualize what’s on the perimeter of the circle? What is outside the boundaries of the circle? Allow
your surroundings to grow around you in your mind’s eye. Describe what you see all around you. What
