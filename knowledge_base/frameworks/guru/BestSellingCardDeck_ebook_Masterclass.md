Secret #1
Be Strategic
Creating a card deck is a fun creative process and it will be a lot more successful if you start with a little
left-brain foundational work.
Your card deck needs to make sense in your business model. There are three things to think about when
it comes to business strategy; who you serve, what they need and how your idea fits with your other
offerings.
CREATE FOR THE PEOPLE YOU SERVE
Sometimes we get a really creative idea but it is for a whole different audience or out of the mainstream
of what we are currently doing. One of the things I tell my clients often is that just because you could
create something, doesn’t mean you should.

lots of fun ideas. As hard as it is - bring a

to pursue. It is so much easier to create

A product flow strategy is like a puzzle. You create your products and programs so that the pieces fit together. They are connected and build on each other. Think about what you offer now. Which offer would
would fit beautifully with a card deck? Or flip that and think about your card deck idea and what type of
class or program could emerge from it. If you don’t have a handle on how a card deck will fit, it’s totally
okay. The ideas will start percolating as we go.

Secret # 2
Take A Stand
A best selling card deck deck will clearly reflect your brand and your message. It’s important to take a
stand and put a stake in the ground for what you believe. Your stand is why you do what you do and how
you advocate for your clients. You want your deck to stand out and clearly speak to your ideal client - no
generic, one size fits all, card decks please.
One of the things I have my clients do to clearly identify that stand is to write a manifesto. You can write
a manifesto as a general reflection of your business and you can write one for each product you create. If
you want a best selling card deck, write a manifesto for it! for example, if you want to create a card deck
about leadership - write a manifesto about leadership. If you want to create a card deck about soul work
- write a manifesto about soul work. It’s so important to be willing to know what you know and take a
stand for it.
Here’s why I advocate for writing a manifesto. It gives you a point of view - your point of view! It gives you
clarity about the stand you take for your clients. It’s a declaration of truths intended to connect with your
ideal client’s heart. It has an emotional quality that will act like a magnet for the right people. And it will
begin to give you content.
When you know what you believe in and what you stand for and create from that space MAGIC happens.
It will guide your choices, contribute to your branding and you will become a powerful advocate for your
ideal clients. They will immediately “get” what you are about and the stand you will take for them. This is
true for your business and it’s true for your products.
Experiment with finding your core message with the worksheet that follows.
To find your ‘stand’, you will begin by playing with two prompts: “What I know…. and What I believe”.
The frame for these questions will be the focus of your core work. For example, if your focus is leadership - the questions would be related to that - “What do I know about leadership?” “What do I believe
about leadership?” You can do this exercise with more than one focus area but only one at a time.
Once you set this intention, you may find answers coming to you while you are cooking a meal, walking
the dog, tending the garden, etc.
If you are a life coach for example, you might identify that you really believe that limiting beliefs are
responsible for the majority of unhappiness and struggle. And you know that actions shift when beliefs
shift. That is just the tip of the iceberg of what this person knows and believes. The content you surface
as you work on a manifesto is a powerful starting place for rock solid content for your deck.
What follows is a manifesto worksheet to get you started.

Secret #3
Content & Process
A card deck is a combination of process & content. It’s important to be conscious of both as you create your deck. For your
deck to be a best seller the content and process need to enhance each other, engage the user and deliver a specific impact.
TYPES OF CARD DECKS
Often people have just one notion of a card deck type and want
to try to make their content fit. That’s a clear path to a watered
down, generic, dare I say boring, type of product. That’s not
what you want and not what I want for you. Begin by exploring
the different card deck types and play with options. Each type
will provide a little bit different way to think about your deck.

Secret #4
Don’t Stop Too Soon
Don’t stop too soon to create a best selling card deck, you must move past the generic and really make it
unique to you and your business. In my experience the best ideas often show up in the 2nd, 3rd or even
4th iteration.
Unfortunately, too many people stop too soon. I can’t tell you how many people come to me with basically the same idea. They want to create a deck with quotes or affirmations paired with their vacation
photos. This is a great starting place - not a great ending place.
From that initial urge, the next step is to look at the concept with your business strategy in mind and
clarity about your stand. So now we are looking at a specific type of affirmation or quote - for example
leadership or creativity or fitness (insert your core focus here). Then consider the impact you want the
deck to have - what need will it address or experience will it provide. Your stand might even have an
emotional quality to it - perhaps your stand is fierce, or maybe you believe humor is important. This will
influence the look and feel of the deck. As you do these things, your deck will begin feel good and feel
right to you. Your authenticity is key to being perceived as unique and original.
Always hold your initial idea loosely and play with options. As you work with the idea, your concept will
evolve. Thats a normal part of the creative process. It will make for a much more successful card deck.
PLAY WITH POSSIBILITIES
As we have already covered, there are a lot of variables to play around with, play is the operative word
here. Play opens up creative flow and makes the process a lot more fun.
You can also begin to play with your content. There are so many ways to approach content that will move
you beyond quotes or affirmations. Not that those are bad, they can be done well. But do play with other options.

Secret #5
The Right Resources
As your idea takes on a shape, feel and direction that excites you will know it’s time to begin reaching out
to the professionals who can make your idea really come alive. The choices you make here are critical to
your deck being a best seller.
Card decks are tactile, visual products and investing sufficiently in the product’s look, packaging and
branding is as critical to success as the time spent on content development.
There are so many talented (and affordable) designers, illustrators and photographers out there that
can make your deck irresistible to your ideal customers. Don’t “cheap out” as you get to this stage of the
creative process.
I receive products all the time from people who either want to sell their product in my online store or
want me to review it for choice Magazine. I can tell you from experience - first impressions matters! This
is true whether your customer first sees your deck on your website, at a conference or in a bookstore.
That first impression is where they make the decision to learn more or move on. It’s that important.
Good design should carry the message and support the desired impact of your deck. You want to find
professionals who are experienced and who “get” the vision you have for your deck.
Here’s what happens when you do not invest sufficiently in design.
I received a card deck all about the laws of attraction and it’s intention was to remind and inspire via
those principles. Great idea, definitely a market for it.
The content was good enough but there was a huge disconnect between the content and the look and
feel of the product. The cards were on white business card sized stock with simple graphic clipart images. There was nothing about the look and feel of the cards that said wealth and abundance. The author
had bailed on their own “stand” and failed to invest sufficiently in their idea. As a card deck it failed to
deliver the desired impact because of the incongruence between the design (or lack of) and the content.
I said no to them. It cost them a distribution avenue and an opportunity to be featured in a professional
magazine.
The other thing you need to outsource is printing and production.
You can do print on demand. There is minimal risk - there is also minimal profit. This is not a bad place
to start as it allows you to test your concept. Customer feedback is invaluable and it allows you to tweak
your product. Once you get the sense that you’ve nailed it, and there is demand and you have worked
out the kinks, THEN take advantage of economies of scale. This refers to the fact that the more you print,

Summary
You absolutely can create a best selling card deck! You can have steady sales - aka passive income. You
can create programs around your deck. You can bundle your deck with other products and processes.
You can get your name out there in new ways. And you will be giving your ideal clients a tool that can
change their life.
To create that best selling card deck you must:
1. Be Strategic. Create for the people you already serve. You already have a relationship and marketing
the deck to them will be easy. Create something that addresses a need or desire and fits with your
other programs.
2. Take A Stand. Create a deck that clearly reflects your brand and your message. It’s important to take
a stand and put a stake in the ground for what you believe. This will make your deck stand out and
clearly speak to your ideal client.
3. Content & Process. For your deck to be a best seller equal attention needs to be given to the content
and the process. They need to enhance each other, engage the user and deliver a specific impact.
There is room to get inventive here.
4. Don’t Stop Too Soon. Developing a card deck is a creative process and it’s often the 2nd, 3rd or 4th
iteration that surfaces the best seller. Play with options and don’t settle for the first idea that pops
up. Play with all of the variables: card deck type, card deck process, content approaches, purpose,
intention and feel.
5. The Right Resources. It’s critical to invest as much energy and resources into production as you do
the concept. Stand behind your idea, find the help you need and make it the best and most beautiful
deck possible. Shelf appeal matters and printing in quantity will give you the best profitability.
Success is a choice! Intention can be a really important part of the inner game. I invite you to think big!
