How to Grow Your Client Base
Before Launching Your Group...

Get Clear on the Purpose of Your Group
Write a through description of the group, including who it’s for (and isn’t for), the
guidelines for the group or do’s and don’ts list, the mission of the group, or any
other pertinent info you want the members to know.
PRO TIP: Include a link to download a free “welcome” gift exclusive to the
members of the group - this way you’re building your email list too!

Create a Pinned Post
....to leave at the top of your group that reiterates the guidelines, welcomes
members, encourages them to introduce themselves, shares first steps to getting
started, and gives further details on the “do’s and dont’s.” (Example “Don’t:” Please
DO NOT SPAM the group with links to your free gift, your site, your FB group, etc.)
PRO TIP: Add a personal touch by including a video welcome message from you
that touches on some of the important points about the group and gives info
about how the members can use it. For bonus points, direct them to a free offer
they need to opt in to receive!

Apply Tags and "Type" to Your Group
...to give more detail about what it’s about (tags example: Entrepreneurship,
Leadership, Business). (Type example: Support) This info shows up at the bottom of
the Group Discussion section. This will also help the "right" people find your group
and understand what it's about.

How to Grow Your Client Base
Before Launching Your Group...

Create an Eye-Catching Cover Photo
..for your group using a tool like Canva, or have one created for you. At a minimum,
include the name of the group in the cover.
PRO TIP: You can include a call to action in the photo to click on it for your free
gift, which will lead them to a post with the clickable link to the page where
they need to opt in to get the gift.

Create the First Week's Worth of Content
This will keep you from scrambling for things to post when your group is still new.
Consider giving each day of the week a theme (Examples: Wins Wednesday, rants,
common mistakes, case studies, recommendations, etc.), and creating a graphic for
each using a tool like Canva. Be sure to include a description in your post of what
the theme means for members.

Upload Helpful Files
...for members in the Files section. These could be a Word doc version of the group
rules, a handy tool, checklist, or template that would be valuable for the members,
or an ongoing updated list of all the members names and info. Get creative based on
what your group’s purpose is.
PRO TIP: In these files, consider pointing people to get even further information
on the topic by sending them to a URL to opt in for more!

How to Grow Your Client Base
Before Launching Your Group...

Set Your Privacy Setting
..to “Secret” UNTIL you are ready to launch. Then change it to “closed”
(recommended over “open”). This allows you to approve people before they join,
and lets others see the group in their feeds (therefore encouraging new members
to join), without seeing the posts.

Invite Your First Batch of People
... to the group by sharing the link with them and telling them to request to join.
Never add people without their permission, it feels icky. Consider your first invites to
be clients, customers, and colleagues, for example.

Use Qualifying Questions
You can require people who request to join to answer "qualifying" questions
before you approve their request. One of these could be to ask if they'd like
additional resources or freebies - so you'll need their email address to send it to
them. Another could ask if they would like to work with you (if they say yes you
message them and take it from there.) Another could ask what their biggest
challenge is, which is always great market research info to have.
PRO TIP: Use a tool like GroupFunnels to automate adding leads to your CRM
and recording answers to the questions.

How to Grow Your Client Base
After Launching Your Group...

Post Often
Your content needs to bring in LEADS. You want to not only start posts, but
comment and engage on the members’ posts. This is the key to sustaining and
growing your group - engagement is hugely important. Especially in the
beginning as you grow, it’s a good idea to post something 1-2x/day. (Daily
themes can help a lot here.) As the group grows, you’ll see members begin to
start threads and help “carry” the group.
PRO TIP: Use a tool like Hootsuite to create an automated posting schedule of
different types of content.

Continue to Invite and Drive People to the Group
Remind members regularly to invite those they feel would be a good fit for the
group to join, and include a P.S. in your emails with a call to action to join.
PRO TIP: Also include an invite to the group in the emails in your follow up
sequence that new subscribers receive once they opt in, as well as on your
"thank you" pages.

Post Your Promos
...and paid offers from time to time and mix them in with the great free value you’re
giving. Hey, it’s your group! :)
PRO TIP: Change your group cover photo to promote what you've got going
on, and create an Event within the group to invite the members to what you
are launching.

How to Grow Your Client Base
After Launching Your Group...

Drive People Elsewhere
Use the group as a way to drive people to other things you have going on, like
livestreams, free webinars, blog posts, and more. This is a HUGE way you can
build your email list AND get clients via your group.

Run a Free Challenge
I've done this many times with great success! In order to join the challenge, they
must opt in to receive the daily challenge emails. Those emails can direct them to
post in the group. You can use the challenge to launch a paid offer at the end.

Provide Lots of Great Value
ALWAYS strive for this, most of all. It will not only encourage members to stick
around, but it will also build know, like, and trust and make it much more likely that
they'll be happy to opt in to your list... and become a client or customer!

How to Grow Your Client Base: Bonus Tips
4 Big Bonus Tips...

Offer Freebies Regularly
This is a great way to add value AND build your list. Ideally, you'll have multiple
freebies on different "hot button" topics that you rotate offering in the group.

Create "Convo Openers"
It's smart to regularly create posts with the intention of driving people to a private
Messenger conversation. Once in the convo, you can drive them to a paid offer or a
phone call with you. For example: "I'm looking for 5 COACHES who want to add
$5k-$10k a month (or more) to their income before the July 4th holiday. Note: You
must already be working with clients, already earning money in your business, and
able to handle fast growth.If that's you, comment 'SCALE' below, and I'll private
message you some details..."

Don't Be Boring!
Although it may seem like the thing you're "supposed" to do, be careful of just
constantly "teaching" in your posts. Mix it up with stories, anecdotes, and short
livestreams, and aim to give people a glimpse at the authentic you. Don't be
afraid to share what makes you different!

Regularly Share Social Proof
Whether it's testimonials, case studies, endorsements, or simple "congrats" to
people as they join your programs, mixing in social proof allows you to take
advanatage of others who "toot your horn."
