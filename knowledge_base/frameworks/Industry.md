# Industry

Ported from the FHS wiki `Industry.md` and related pages.

## Highland Games & Pipe Bands

- **Canadian piping/drumming competitions** are sanctioned by the Pipe Bands of Scotland (PBS) and associated national societies (Pipe Bands of Canada, Pipe Bands of Alberta, etc.).
- **Highland Games** are the primary marketing venue — they happen May through September, with major events across Western Canada.
- **Competition season** drives consumable sales (reeds, chanter reeds, drone reeds, practice chanters, bag maintenance supplies).
- **Pipe Bands** are cyclical: membership fluctuates; new bands form; some disband each year.
- **Solo pipers/drummers** compete year-round in local/regional events, with peaks around major games.

### Key associations

- Pipe Bands of Canada (national)
- Pipe Bands of Alberta (provincial)
- Highland Games & Dance Festival Associations (provincial)
- Scottish Country Dance Society (for dancers)

## Highland Dance

- **Competition year-round** (unlike piping/drumming which is seasonal).
- Major events: Canadian Highland Games Championships, provincial championships.
- **Age groups**: Juvenile (under 12), Junior (12–16), Senior (17+), Adult, Masters.
- **Costumes** are standardized for competition (regulated by the World Highland Fling Festival or similar bodies).
- **Teachers/studios** are key buyers — they order costumes for students, bulk discounts matter.

## Market Dynamics

- **Niche market**: Limited number of serious practitioners in North America.
- **Community-driven**: Education, mentorship, and word-of-mouth are critical.
- **Heritage connection**: Customers buy to connect with Scottish ancestry or culture.
- **High switching costs**: Once a customer buys a bagpipe, they’ll likely buy accessories, parts, and consumables from the same vendor for life.

## Trends

- Growing interest in Scottish culture (Tartan Day, Burns Night, genealogy).
- Online learning (YouTube, digital chanters) increasing accessibility.
- Demand for high-quality instruments is rising; synthetic materials (poly, carbon fiber) gaining acceptance.
- Social media amplifying community content (TikTok, Instagram Reels).
