# Ask Method Framework

## Overview

The ASK Method is a systematic approach to discovering what people want before they even know it themselves, then delivering custom experiences that convert.

## Core Methodology

### The Three-Step Process

```mermaid
graph LR
    A[DISCOVER] --> B[SEGMENT]
    B --> C[LAUNCH]
```

### 1. DISCOVER
**Problem**: People don't know what they want  
**Solution**: Ask them what they DON'T want

#### Deep Dive Survey
- Uses **SMIQ (Single Most Important Question)**
- Format: `"When it comes to X, what's your single biggest challenge or frustration?"`
- Focuses on pain points instead of desires
- Reveals hidden market segments

### 2. SEGMENT
**Problem**: Standard niches aren't specific enough  
**Solution**: Find the different buckets within your market

#### Quiz Funnel
- Series of multiple-choice questions
- Activates 4 Universal Psychological Triggers:
  1. **Micro-Commitments** - Small steps lead to bigger actions
  2. **Self-Discovery** - People love learning about themselves
  3. **Curiosity** - Intrigue drives engagement
  4. **Desire** - Segmentation creates specific desires

### 3. LAUNCH
**Problem**: Generic offers don't convert  
**Solution**: Custom experiences for each segment

#### Custom Experience Delivery
- Tailored messaging per market bucket
- Specific solution prescriptions
- Personalized path through funnel

## Case Study: Orchid Care Market
- **Initial assumption**: Single target audience
- **Reality discovered**: 4 distinct buckets
  1. People who want their orchid to bloom again
  2. People who want to save a sick or dying orchid
  3. People who need help potting/transplanting their orchids
  4. People who just bought their first orchid and need basics
- **Result**: $0 to $25,000/month in 18 months

## Implementation Framework

### Phase 1: Discovery Setup
1. Define your X (market focus)
2. Craft your SMIQ
3. Create survey with open-ended responses
4. Deploy to existing audience

### Phase 2: Analysis & Segmentation
1. Review survey responses for patterns
2. Identify 3-5 distinct market buckets
3. Create segment personas
4. Map pain points to solutions

### Phase 3: Quiz Development
1. Write 8-12 multiple-choice questions
2. Map answers to segments
3. Design quiz landing page
4. Integrate psychological triggers

### Phase 4: Custom Experience
1. Create unique offers per segment
2. Develop segment-specific messaging
3. Build automated segmentation workflows
4. Test and optimize conversion paths

## Psychological Triggers Activation

| Trigger | How to Activate | Expected Outcome |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| Micro-Commitments | Small quiz questions | Higher conversion rates |
| Self-Discovery | Personality-style questions | Increased engagement |
| Curiosity | Progressive revelation | Time-on-page growth |
| Desire | Custom results | Purchase intent lift |
