## Grant Cardone Sales System

### The 10-Stage Sales Cycle

1. **Awareness** — Make prospects aware of a need
2. **Interest** — Customer researches options
3. **Engage (Greet)** — Attract or greet; book meeting
4. **Subscribe** — Join mailing list/follow page
5. **Consider (Needs/Wants)** — Determine requirements
6. **Intent (Presentation/Proposal)** — Present solution
7. **Evaluation** — Customer decides
8. **Convert (Close)** — Make the sale
9. **Experience** — Deliver product/service
10. **Ascend/Advocate** — Grow relationship, get referrals

### Key Insights

- **Shorten the cycle** — Don't talk yourself out of the sale
- **Always propose** — The client won't just hand you cash
- **Objections are predictable** — Prepare responses in advance
- **Only 2 real objections**:
  1. They aren't the decision maker
  2. They don't think it will work

### Marketing Principles

- **Money follows attention** — Generate leads first
- **Marketing is systematic** — Processes, not random events
- **Social media downtime plan**:
  - Blog posts
  - Email campaigns
  - Direct mail
  - Cold calling
  - Referral requests

## Dan Lok Traffic Conversion Secrets

### 5 Quick-Turn Tactics for BIG Ticket Deals

1. **Compare apples to oranges** — Show multiple functions in one product
2. **Decrease price by adding value components** — Free additions that increase perceived value
3. **Identify unique, invaluable aspect** — What makes you different from 98% average competitors
4. **Make high price itself a benefit** — Premium = quality (organic foods)
5. **Break down big amounts into smaller parts** — "Less than a cup of coffee a day"

### Key Insight

Value differences come from expertise and service (exclusivity, access, guarantee), not just core product. It's easier to sell half at double price than compete on lowest price.

## Grant Cardone Money Rules

### 10 Commandments of Money

1. **Never Spend Until You Get It** — Money isn't in the bank until it's there. Deal could fall through.
2. **40% Rule** — 40% taxes, 40% pay yourself (marketing/advertising), 20% living expenses
3. **If You Can't Write It Off, Don't Buy It** — Business purchases as investments
4. **Rent and Lease, Don't Own** — Keep liquidity
5. **Stabilize First Flow Before Second** — Focus on main business before side hustles
6. **47 Rule** — 7 hours/week on yourself (read, mentors, coaches)
7. **Always Stay Broke** — Cash is trash; put money to work immediately
8. **Never Lose Money** — Avoid inflation, negative rates, compounding loss
9. **Seek Cashflow, Not Cash** — Money in motion vs. money in bank
10. **Never Quit Until You Achieve Goals** — Faith in execution, not just vision

### Money Principles

- **Cashflow > Cash** — Money should be working
- **Compound Interest** — Reinvest profits to grow exponentially
- **Negative Interest Rates** — Prepare for cash erosion

## Elegant Follow-Up Technique (Issamar Ginzberg)

### The Simple Email Trick

**Problem**: How to follow up without seeming pushy?

**Solution**: Email elegantly a second time by:
1. Research client's industry via Google News
2. Find relevant news article
3. Email them the link (don't mention previous email)

**Result**: Often get "thank you" that mentions previous email

### HARO Application

For media pitches:
1. Email reporter with pitch
2. Research topic-relevant news
3. Email additional article: "In addition to my pitch, I found this article you might find relevant..."

**Boomerang effect**: Only respondent with two shots at mention

## Social Media Strategies

### 3 Message Formula (Cold Outreach)

**Message #1 - Relationship Building**
```
"Hey John, what's up? I noticed we're friends here on Facebook and we haven't chatted for a while. I noticed that your profile says you're living up in New York. Is that correct? You up in NY?"
```

**Message #2 - Value Proposition**
```
"John's that's awesome, I visited New York for the first time last September and I loved it. I was actually hoping you said you were still there, because I have a business project that I'm expanding and I'm doing some networking in your neck of the woods. Would you at all be open to connecting more regarding a side income project, if it didn't interfere with what you're already doing?"
```

**Message #3 - Meeting Setup**
```
"That's great. Shoot me a few times that you're free this week for about 15 mins and can have access to the internet. Tuesday and Thursday are the best for me if you can make something work either of those days."
```

## Sales Funnel

### 3 Types of Funnels

**Determine which to use by asking**:
1. Large prospect list but few buyers? → Acquisition funnel
2. Need more leads? → Acquisition funnel
3. Optimized backend, want revenue? → Monetization funnel

### Acquisition Funnel
Goal: Acquire prospects and customers at breakeven

**Types**:
- Free Book Funnel
- Football Phone Funnel
- Trial Upgrade Funnel
- Value In Advance Funnel
- Splinter Offer Funnel

**Requirements**:
- Customer Avatars defined
- Customer Value Optimization mapped
- Lead Magnet created

### Activation Funnel
Goal: Convert prospects/inactive buyers into recent buyers

**Types**:
- Simple Survey Funnel
- Velvet Rope Funnel
- Blog Launch Funnel
- Flash Sale Funnel

**Triggers**:
- Price increase
- Limited quantity
- Sale

### Monetization Funnel
Goal: Generate revenue from active buyers/subscribers

**Types**:
- Perpetual Webinar Formula
- Mini-Class Launch
- Paid Webinar Funnel
- Live Event Funnel
- Magic Question Funnel ("Want some help with X?")

### Typical Funnel Steps

1. Pay for Ads
2. Send to Landing Page for email
3. Give freebie/webinar
4. Email follow-up
5. (Optional) Join FB Group
6. Book call
7. Talk
8. Convert

### Funnel Components

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Reality Check | Set benchmarks, do math |
| Demand | Target best conversion segment |
| The Goods | Irresistible offer |
| Idea/Map | Unique positioning |
| The Beacon | Right traffic direction |

### Josh Turner Anti-Funnel

Alternative approach (no landing page):
1. Find Prospects
2. Daily Posts
3. Relevant Content
4. Social Media
5. Stay top of mind
6. Thought Leadership
7. 5-7 emails
8. Helpful resources
9. Direct Message
10. Build Trust → Chat
