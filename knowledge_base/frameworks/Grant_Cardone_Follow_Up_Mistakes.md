# Grant Cardone 10 Biggest Follow-Up Mistakes

## Core Philosophy
Follow-up is the single most important element of closing sales. Most sales are made between the 5th and 12th contact, yet 80% of salespeople don't make enough calls.

## The 10 Mistakes

### 1. Not Making the Call
- **The Problem**: 48% of all salespeople today don't make the call
- **The Solution**: Always make the call regardless of excuses
- **Key Quote**: "I don't care what you say, if you don't know what they told you they aren't going to be home—MAKE THE CALL"
- **Action**: If they're out of town? Call them. If they're in a meeting? Call them. Make the call.

### 2. Not Enough Contacts, Frequency, Volume
- **The Problem**: Not making enough phone calls, emails, or texts
- **The Solution**: Need 5-12 contacts, not 1-2
- **Key Stat**: 80% of all sales are made between the 5th and 12th contact
- **Action**: Make enough calls to ensure multiple touchpoints with prospects

### 3. Calls Not on a Regular Basis
- **The Problem**: Random calling patterns - one call now, another in 3 months
- **The Solution**: Consistent, regular follow-up schedule
- **Key Quote**: "With follow-up, better than perfect, not another rock" - GC
- **Action**: Create a disciplined calling schedule and stick to it

### 4. Waiting Too Long
- **The Problem**: Delaying follow-up reduces chances of conversion by ~320%
- **The Solution**: Follow up immediately and consistently
- **Key Stat**: Speed increases chances of conversion by over 300%
- **Action**: Don't wait - follow up fast and regularly

### 5. Lacking Variety in Reasons to Follow Up
- **The Problem**: Repeating the same follow-up approach leads to boredom and predictability
- **The Solution**: Use a full arsenal of follow-up methods
- **Methods to Use**:
  - Text messages
  - Email
  - Handwritten notes
  - Phone calls
  - Personal visits
- **Key Principle**: It's for the prospect, not for you - use whatever works

### 6. No Clear Purpose for the Call
- **The Problem**: Calling without a clear reason or purpose
- **The Solution**: Always have a clear intent before calling
- **Key Quote**: "Hey, there's reason I'm calling..." - present purpose upfront
- **Action**: State your purpose clearly at the beginning of every call

### 7. Not Looking Desperate Enough
- **The Problem**: Hiding desperation or eagerness for business
- **The Solution**: Be openly hungry for business
- **Key Quote**: "I'm trying to grow my business - I'm desperate. Every time I call someone, I leave a message."
- **Action**: Always leave messages, be persistent, show you want their business

### 8. Not Collecting Critical Data
- **The Problem**: Failing to collect and track critical prospect information
- **The Solution**: Systematically collect, log, and track all relevant data
- **Key Principle**: Collect data, log it, look for it, have it
- **Action**: Track buying cycles, investment patterns, and future opportunities

### 9. Not Asking for Referrals
- **The Problem**: Failing to ask existing contacts for referrals
- **The Solution**: Systematically ask for referrals from everyone
- **Key Quote**: "Who do you know that would benefit from my product or service?"
- **Action**: Ask frequently, ask everyone, ask for referrals regularly

### 10. Not Organizing and Maintaining Data
- **The Problem**: No CRM or data management system in place
- **The Solution**: Keep all contact data organized and updated
- **Key Principle**: Contacts or contracts - always keep the data, always update them
- **Action**: Implement a CRM system, keep data current, maintain organized records
