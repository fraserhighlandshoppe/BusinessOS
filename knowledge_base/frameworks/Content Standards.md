# Content Standards

## Blogging Mistakes

### Common Mistakes with Expert Insights

**1. Not making it clear what your blog is about**
- Users can't figure out the site in 3-5 seconds
- Impact: Can't get email signups or sales

**2. Not communicating what's going to happen**
- Fear of unknown prevents email/credit card sharing
- Solution: Set expectations upfront

**3. Making people feel like you've gone back to 1999**
- Old design = unprofessional
- Good design is enough

**4. Not showing people how secure you are**
- Use PayPal, Visa/MasterCard logos
- "Secured by" messaging

**5. Making people jump through hoops**
- More clicks = fewer sales
- Only ask for necessary info

**6. Breaking down before their very eyes**
- Sales process must be bulletproof
- Reliability, validation, accessibility

**7. Not showing safety in numbers**
- 10,000 happy customers = low-risk purchase
- Show real numbers

**8. Not showing past or future**
- For long sales processes, show journey
- Keeps users motivated

**9. Asking for too much too soon**
- Passwords = bank account access
- Ask after proving worth

**10. Looking, talking, thinking small**
- Show good company associations
- Be small but strategically

### 400-Word Formula

**Introduction**: 120 words (3 sentences)
1. Introduce topic
2. Introduce problem
3. Explain solution

**Body**: 7 bullet points, 40 words each (280 words)

**Summary**: 48 words
- Recap what was covered

**Total**: ~448 words

### Keyword Strategy

Select keywords from 3 categories:
1. **Broad**: 1 keyword
2. **Targeted**: 2 keywords
3. **Long Tail**: 1 keyword

### Article Structure for Syndication

1. **Title**: Attention-grabbing, includes greed factor
2. **Introduction**: 100 words setting up the article
3. **Body**: 3-7 main points, each as paragraph
4. **Summary**: Recap key points
5. **Resource Box**: Strong CTA, benefit-focused

### Best Practices

- **Length**: 1,000-1,200 words for syndication
- **Entertainment**: Humor and controversy increase pickup
- **No Direct Selling**: Focus on value, not promotion
- **Follow-up**: Track syndications and offer further work

### EZA (EzineArticles) Strategy

1. Create original article on your site first
2. Install second blog for summaries with links
3. Bookmark and submit RSS feeds
4. Rewrite 35%+ for different directories
5. Cross-link resource boxes
6. Create PDF for document sharing sites
7. Rinse and repeat

### Article Marketing Formula

1. Title: Get attention
2. Intro: Set up expectations
3. Body: Deliver on promises
4. Summary: Reinforce key points
5. Resource Box: Drive traffic

## Copywriting Standards

### Headline Formulas

1. How To [Result] In [Time]
2. Secrets of [Success]
3. Give Me [Time] Minutes
4. [Number] Ways To [Goal]
5. The News Headline
6. The Testimonial
7. The Offer
8. The FREE headline

### Email Writing

- No apologies
- Specific benefits
- Clear CTA
- Personalized greeting

### Social Media Posts

- 4-part formula: Hook → Story → Takeaway → CTA
- 80/20 rule: 80% value, 20% promotion
- Never link directly - ask for private message

## Content Repurposing

### The 7 Magic Content Pieces

1. **Video** (interviews, webinars, live videos)
2. **Audio** (podcasts, audiobooks)
3. **Text** (blogs, eBooks, stats, testimonials)

### Repurposing Formula
1 blog post → 44 social media posts across 6 platforms

### Best Practices

| Type | Tips |
|---|---|
| Headlines | Use numbers, "will make you", "this is why" |
| Image Quotes | People images (+95% conversion), 25 words max |
| Video | Phone filming OK, window lighting, selfie-style |
| Editing | Free tools or hire editor |
| Final | Square/portrait format, captions required |
