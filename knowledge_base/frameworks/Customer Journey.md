# Customer Journey & Sales Cycle

Ported from the FHS wiki `FHS Sales Cycle and Customer Journey`. This blends
Grant Cardone's sales cycle, Digital Marketer's customer journey, and Mautic's
journey map. It is the canonical funnel for all FHS marketing/agents.

## Stages

1. **Awareness (Attention)** — make prospects aware of product/service in a way
   that addresses a need. Tools: social profiles/posts/interactions, website,
   mailing list, TV/radio, print, bulk mail, door hangers, word of mouth.
2. **Interest** — customer aware of need; researches/compares. Tools: website
   product info, short/long copy with benefits, social posts, email newsletter.
3. **Engage (Greet)** — attract (digital) or greet (in person); book initial
   meeting. Tools: email list, direct email, social, FB Messenger. Exception:
   if not a fit / no rapport/trust, they don't progress.
4. **Subscribe** — subscribe to mailing list or follow page (or hardcopy list);
   added to one+ campaigns. Tools: website landing page, social posts, flyers.
   Exception: may unsubscribe.
5. **Consider (Needs and Wants)** — determine needs/wants to build a proposal.
   Tools: questions/questionnaire, KYC, Financial Needs Assessment.
6. **Intent (Presentation/Proposal)** — firm up intention; present solution
   addressing identified needs and building value. Tools: proposal (product
   info + benefits), agent support. Exception: questionnaire may surface
   negative emotions — prepare the client.
7. **Evaluation** — customer decides: accept whole / part / reject / buy
   something else. Tools: agent support, objection handling. Exceptions:
   rejection or partial acceptance means re-evaluate needs.
8. **Convert / Purchase (Close)** — purchase. Upsell (digital) / cross-sell
   (both). Tools: customer support (applications, follow-up). Exception: client
   may ghost (uncertainty the solution works).
9. **Experience** — deliver product/service; best possible experience. Tools:
   customer/tech support, outreach (satisfaction, remind to use), reason-why
   letter. Exceptions: RATED or DECLINED → more support needed.
10. **Excite** — keep client excited about purchase. Tools: outreach
    (satisfaction, remind of benefits).
11. **Ascend** — grow the relationship / value.
12. **Advocate** — turn into a raving fan. Tools: outreach, shareable materials.
13. **Promote** — client promotes FHS via referrals / affiliate. Tools: agent
    support, affiliate program, shareable materials.

## Principles

- Touchpoints create friction; friction can derail journeys or cause negative
  emotion. Design touchpoints for a positive experience — especially in
  exceptions.
- Marketing's job is to trigger ACTION and create perceived value; education
  over selling.
- Blend of Grant Cardone (Attention → Greet → Needs/Wants → Presentation →
  Proposal → Close) and Digital Marketer / Mautic journey maps.

## Mapping to channels

- Awareness/Interest/Engage/Subscribe → social media (`content/Channels/`), blog,
  SEO, events (Highland Games).
- Consider/Intent/Evaluation → email/newsletter, direct consult, website.
- Convert → WooCommerce / Square / in-person / Games booth.
- Experience→Promote → email follow-up, referral program, reviews/testimonials.
