# Ali Brown Copywriting Action Plan Framework

## Overview

This framework provides 7 core strategies for crafting high-converting copy across all marketing channels, emphasizing audience connection and business results.

## Core Strategies

### 1. Understanding Copy Importance
- Copy is the DNA of all marketing materials
- Without words, ads lose their persuasive power
- Every medium requires carefully crafted messaging
- **Action**: Review all business copy and assess effectiveness

### 2. Audience Definition
- Never target "everyone" - leads to watered down messaging
- Identify ideal client personas with specific characteristics
- Focus on prequalified prospects who can invest
- **Action**: List marketing efforts and audience alignment

### 3. Hot Buttons Discovery
- Use POWER point questions to identify motivations
- Understand what keeps prospects awake at night
- Connect on their terms, not yours
- **Action**: Validate hot buttons with surveys/testimonials

### 4. Ego Management
- Customers care about benefits to them, not your accomplishments
- Use credentials strategically (bios, not sales copy)
- Focus on customer transformation and results
- **Action**: Audit copy for self-focus vs. customer-focus

### 5. Offer Integration
- Every piece needs clear call-to-action
- Direct response beats clever wordplay for sales
- Define end-result before writing
- **Action**: Map CTAs to each marketing channel

### 6. Storytelling Framework
- Facts tell, stories sell
- Balance facts/stats with emotional connection
- Position product as path to better life
- **Action**: Create story bank tied to core audience desires

### 7. Channel Consideration
- Match copy length/format to delivery method
- Radio/TV vs. print vs. digital requirements differ
- Long vs. short copy decisions based on audience qualification
- **Action**: Outline delivery methods and copy adaptations

## Copy Investment Decision Framework

### DIY vs. Professional Decision Tree
- Evaluate ROI of copy investment
- Consider training costs vs. hiring costs
- Factor in time-to-market for campaigns

## Project Prioritization Matrix

### Online Copy Requirements
- Downloadable reports/lead magnets
- Website copy and navigation
- Landing pages and sales letters
- Autoresponders and email sequences
- Blog content and editorial calendars

### Offline Copy Requirements
- Print ads and brochures
- Direct mail campaigns
- Radio/TV scripts
- Business card and signage content

## Application to Copywriter Agent

When the Copywriter Agent receives a task:
1. Identify which of the 7 strategies applies to the request
2. Apply appropriate format (storytelling for social, direct for ads)
3. Ensure ego-free, customer-benefit-focused messaging
4. Include clear call-to-action for the specific channel
5. Match length/format to channel requirements
