# Customer Avatars

Ported from the FHS wiki `FHS Marketing Plan` (Ideal Customer sections). The
core principle from the wiki: *customers hate to be sold but love to buy — we
must educate, not sell.* Understand the customer better than they understand
themselves (desires, fears, fantasies).

## Avatar 1 — Pipers and Drummers (individuals)

Note: pipe-band members often have everyday items (reeds, etc.) provided by the
band, so target the individual player and the band separately.

- Age: 10+
- Gender: male and female
- Income: varies (disposable / discretionary)
- Decision-maker: teens → joint with parents (band/PM influence); adults → the
  player (band/PM influence); band members → PM/PS/DS/band council.
- Psychographics:
  - Brand consciousness higher for serious competitors.
  - Buy on: price (we are competitive/slightly lower — lower overhead),
    availability (we keep stock others don't), prior experience, recommendations
    (friends/instructors/internet/staff).
  - Middle-to-upper class; a "dirt poor bagpiper" usually has a sponsor.
  - Emotional drivers: ability to afford, need for competition/performance.
  - Influencers: instructors, previous experience, band members, Pipe Major,
    internet.
- Geographics: primarily Southern Alberta. Can sell worldwide, but mail order
  outside Canada makes less sense.

## Avatar 2 — Highland Dancers (individuals)

- Age: 15–30 (competition age); majority female.
- Income: $20,000+; $500+ discretionary for dance items.
- Decision-maker: parent and/or dancer (and teacher for studios).
- Psychographics: competitive spirit; participates in competitions, games,
  performances, medal tests. Standardized products (SOBHD regulated). Buys on
  convenience, competitive pricing, availability. Values: value for money,
  quality, follows regulations. Influencers: internet, SOBHD, instructors.

## Avatar 3 — Pipe Bands (organizations)

- Age range 6–75; male and female.
- Decision-maker: PM / Pipe Sergeant / Drum Sergeant / Band Council.
- Needs: band-kit lockers, bulk discounts, group outfitting, reeds/consumables.
- Geographics: Alberta and Saskatchewan (bands registered with their societies;
  many unregistered soloists exist too).

## Avatar 4 — Dance Teachers / Studios (organizations)

- Age of proprietor 15–45 (skew female); members 5–25.
- Funded by student/membership fees.
- Decision-maker: teacher, parent, dancer.
- Needs: standardized costuming, reliable supply, bulk/Studio pricing.
- Geographics: English-speaking North America.

## Secondary — Giftware & Highland Wear (impulse buyers)

- Age 15–45; income under $50,000; both genders.
- Affiliations: friends/relatives of dancers, bands, studios.
- Behavior: more likely to buy in person than online; novelty/souvenir/
  heritage purchases.
- Geographics: Southern Alberta (in-person); Canada/world (online).

## Generational notes (from wiki)

- Silent Generation: pre-internet tactics (direct mail, face-to-face).
- Boomers: mix of traditional + online; value youth/individuality.
- Gen X: tech-comfortable; love info + feedback; value learning/growth.
- Millennials: value speed; Twitter/social; blogs > articles; value family.
