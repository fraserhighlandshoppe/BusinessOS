# Grant Cardone 20 Rules of Closing

## Core Philosophy
The closer doesn't quit until they close. Persistence and a complete arsenal of techniques separate great closers from average sellers.

## The 20 Rules

### 01. Always Remain Seated
- Present on your feet, close from your seat
- Keep both yourself and the buyer seated during closes
- Standing suggests something has changed and allows exit
- Stay calm, enforce confidence in reaching agreement

### 02. Always Present Proposal in Writing
- People believe what they see, not what they hear
- "Talk is cheap" - written proposals are more credible
- Always have a contract and writing pad available
- Write down all points of value, even standard inclusions
- Use written agreements as closing points

### 03. Always Clearly Communicate Your Proposal
- Rehearse and clearly communicate all offer terms
- Speak clearly and confidently so communication reaches the buyer
- Practice delivery until you can present with complete confidence
- Use recordings and video to ensure confident presentation

### 04. Always Maintain Eye Contact
- Discipline to maintain eye contact throughout the close
- Avoid wandering eyes, looking around, or over their shoulders
- Eye contact suggests interest in them and confidence in yourself
- Practice with recordings to improve eye contact discipline

### 05. Always Have a Pen Available for Signing
- "Always Be Closing" fails without pen and contract ready
- Keep pen and contract readily available at all times
- Invest in nice professional pens that portray you as a pro
- Keep backup pens and electronic signatures ready

### 06. Use Humor to Relieve Pressure
- Humor is an art used by all great closers
- Use humor that tells a story, makes your point, and relieves pressure
- Avoid humor that makes fun of others
- Use humor that makes buyer feel good, positive, and hopeful
- People pay more for entertainment than education
- Example: "Better to live rich than die rich"

### 07. Always Ask One More Time
- Persistence separates closers from sellers
- Requires deep arsenal of techniques and complete understanding of closes
- Most prospects close after five attempts, average salesperson has only four closes
- Want hundreds of closes available "just in case"
- Practice persistence until professionally smooth and confident

### 08. Always Have Arsenal of Closes Available
- Most salespeople quit due to lack of close variations
- Need variety to handle different customer types and objections
- Most prospects close after ~5 attempts, average salesperson has only 4 closes
- Master through repetition - know them so well you can use in any order
- Listen to audio programs to duplicate every word

### 09. Always Stay With Buyer and Reduce Time Alone
- Create and leverage credibility throughout negotiations
- Each time you leave buyer creates doubt and uncertainty
- Avoid back-and-forth checking with managers
- Appropriate use of authority can be powerful when not overused

### 10. Always Treat Prospect Like a Buyer
- Don't determine who is/isn't a buyer
- Every person who reads this guide can close multiple times
- Survey prospect for signs they've bought before (watch, car, house, credit card)
- Regardless of circumstances, always treat as a buyer
- "Every buyer is a buyer. Treat them as a buyer and they will turn into a buyer."

### 11. Always Know You Can Come to Agreement
- Mental attitude that must be protected like gold at Fort Knox
- "Where there's a will, there's a way"
- Eliminate negativity from environment like a disease that kills
- Newspapers, TV, radio spread misinformation and mental contamination
- Maintain positive mental attitude throughout the close

### 12. Always Maintain Positive Demeanor
- Maintain positive attitude regardless of buyer response or management reaction
- Keep it light, stay positive, maintain professional can-do attitude
- When buyer is negative and you stay positive, you look that much better
- Negativity always succumbs to positivity
- Seasoned professionals must practice this more than anyone

### 13. Always Smile No Matter the Outcome
- Physical manifestation of internal positive attitude
- Practice smiling in every situation: arguments, disagreements, poor service
- "Smile big and smile always"
- Practice until you can argue, disagree, negotiate, overcome objections and close with a smile
- Successful people smile all the time - it's the reason they got successful

### 14. Always Treat Buyer Like They Can
- "If you treat someone like they have money they will act like they have money"
- All words, responses, actions, facial expressions, objection handling must communicate buyer is going to buy
- Treat 'em like they are going to play and they will play
- Every buyer is a buyer - treat them as such every time

### 15. Always Acknowledge Buyer for Any Offer
- Regardless of what buyer offers, even if unacceptable, acknowledge them
- Rookie closer doesn't take time to acknowledge prospect's offer
- When you don't acknowledge, buyer feels unheard and becomes more resistant
- Say "thank you, I appreciate that, and I want to congratulate you for making this suggestion"
- Act like you are not hearing the customer and smallest points become deal killers

### 16. Always Agree with the Buyer
- Different from "customer is always right" - it's about agreement
- Disagreement requires two sides - once one agrees, disagreement is over
- Disagreement only results in not closing deals
- "Lose the battle, win the war"

### 17. Always Look for a Solution
- World is filled with "can't do" people vs "can do" people
- Those that can do, do. Those that can't, don't.
- No one cares what you can't do - only what you can do and how it can be done
- "How can we make this work? What can you do to make this right? Help me out here..."
- People appreciate those who never give up, who always look for solutions

### 18. Care So Much You Refuse Not to Close
- Beyond selling - more on the line of purpose
- Align selling/closing with higher purpose
- Your true purpose will carry you through
- Difference between calls is amount of care communicated in helping people
- Care more and you will close more

### 19. Use Full Arsenal of Closes
- Never depend on or get comfortable with one or two closes
- Be versed enough to use them all
- There's a time to be surgical and times to hammer down
- Master every tool available, become confident using them all
- Just because a close doesn't work on you doesn't mean it won't work on the customer

### 20. Always Know You Don't Provide Service Until You Close
- Selling, presenting, demonstrating, promoting, building trust are all necessary but don't compare to closing
- Closing is the first time you benefit the customer
- Close allows you and company to expand
- All things prior to close were necessary to get to close but don't allow expansion/survival
- Only when customer closes do they benefit from your service or offer
