# Marketing Principles

Core marketing principles distilled from FHS wiki and industry best practices.

## Sales & Marketing Fundamentals

### Grant Cardone Sales Cycle
The 10-stage sales cycle adapted for FHS:

1. **Awareness (Attention)** — Make prospects aware of a need
   - Marketing job: TRIGGER ACTION
   - Tools: Social media, SEO, content marketing
   - Principle: Customers hate to be sold, love to buy

2. **Interest** — Customer researches options
   - Tools: Product info, comparison guides, reviews
   - Principle: Education over selling

3. **Engage (Greet)** — Attract or greet; book meeting
   - Tools: Email list, social media, Messenger
   - Principle: Trust and rapport required

4. **Subscribe** — Join mailing list/follow page
   - Tools: Website forms, social posts, flyers
   - Principle: Permission-based marketing

5. **Consider (Needs/Wants)** — Determine requirements
   - Tools: Questionnaires, needs assessment
   - Principle: Understand before presenting

6. **Intent (Presentation/Proposal)** — Present solution
   - Tools: Proposals, product demonstrations
   - Principle: Build value, not just features

7. **Evaluation** — Customer decides
   - Tools: Objection handling, testimonials
   - Principle: Expect objections; prepare responses

8. **Convert (Close)** — Make the sale
   - Tools: Customer support, follow-up
   - Principle: Close with confidence, not desperation

9. **Experience** — Deliver product/service
   - Tools: Support, satisfaction outreach
   - Principle: Exceed expectations

10. **Ascend/Advocate** — Grow relationship
    - Tools: Referral program, shareable content
    - Principle: Turn customers into marketers

## Customer Psychology

### The FHS Customer Mindset

**Primary Motivators:**
- **Heritage Connection**: Buying to connect with Scottish ancestry/culture
- **Community Belonging**: Part of piping/drumming/dance community
- **Performance Achievement**: Competition success, skill development
- **Identity Expression**: Highland dress as personal expression

**Decision Process:**
1. Emotional trigger (competition, heritage event, gift)
2. Information search (instructor, online, friends)
3. Comparison (price, availability, trust)
4. Purchase (convenience, trust, recommendation)

### Trust Building Principles

1. **Practitioner Authority** — Owner is active Pipe Major/drummer
2. **Availability** — Stock when others don't
3. **Education** — Teach, don't just sell
4. **Community Presence** — Visible at games, events

## Content Marketing Principles

### The 70/30 Rule
- **70% Value-Added Content** — Education, entertainment, inspiration
- **30% Promotional Content** — Products, offers, sales

### Content Types by Stage

| Stage | Content Type | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Educational posts, event coverage | Build awareness |
| Interest | Product demos, comparisons | Drive interest |
| Consider | Reviews, tutorials, FAQs | Build trust |
| Convert | Offers, limited-time deals | Drive purchase |
| Advocate | Testimonials, referral program | Drive referrals |

## Pricing Strategies

### Competitive Positioning
- **Low Overhead = Pricing Flexibility**
- Monitor competitor prices weekly
- Match or beat on key items
- Bundle for perceived value

### Psychological Pricing
- Use $99 instead of $100
- Create bundle pricing (kit deals)
- Limited-time offers create urgency

## Customer Retention

### The Loyalty Loop
1. **Purchase** → Satisfied with experience
2. **Follow-up** → Check satisfaction, request review
3. **Engagement** → Share content, refer friends
4. **Repeat** → Loyal customer returns

### Retention Tactics
- Referral programs
- Seasonal check-ins
- Exclusive access for loyal customers
- Personal outreach for big purchases

## Marketing Automation Framework

### Lead Nurturing Sequence
1. **Day 1**: Welcome + 10% off
2. **Day 3**: Our story + testimonials
3. **Day 7**: Product recommendations
4. **Day 14**: Educational content
5. **Day 30**: Re-engagement offer

### Trigger-Based Campaigns
- **Cart Abandonment**: 1 hour → reminder, 24 hours → discount
- **Post-Purchase**: Day 3 → thank you, Day 14 → accessories
- **Inactive**: 60 days → re-engagement, 90 days → win-back
