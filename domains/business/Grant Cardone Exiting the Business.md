# Grant Cardone Exiting the Business

## The Reality
Most people don't think about selling until under stress and pressure - then they're desperate and can't get true value.

## Types of Exit
1. **Planned**
2. **Forced**

**Statistic**: 2/3 of businesses go out within 5 years.

## Business Value Truth
- Never worth to buyer if you can't exit it
- No exit plan = forced exit = no value

**If you don't exit correctly, everyone who came with you is forced to exit too.**

## Intentional Exit Strategy

### Day 1 Thinking
You need to think about exit from day 1, every day.

**If you can't run business without you, you MUST be there every day.**

### Forces Exit Planning
Thinking about running without you forces consideration of:
- How to hire
- How to delegate

**Business vs Hobby**: If built only because you love it = hobby, not business.

### Commitment to Others
Exit plan shows commitment to:
- Customers
- Employees
- Capital groups
- Family

## Growing Your Business
Model → Mimic → Master → Multiply

## Staffing (Building the Team)
Business is NOT doing the work - it's building the team:
1. **Attracting**
2. **Finding** (selecting)
3. **Aligning**
4. **Developing**
5. **Retain**

## Exiting Process
1. **Transfer knowledge** to replacement
2. **Run parallel** while they learn
3. **Disengage** once competent
4. **Move on** to next role

**Without exit plan = hobby, not business.**

## Planning the Exit

### Target Questions
- How many units sold/controlled for retirement?
- What you look for = what you get
- Can't create freedom one transaction at a time (unless HUGE)

### Strategy
- Follow those who've been through process
- Set targets
- Work the target

**Potential limited only by confidence.**
