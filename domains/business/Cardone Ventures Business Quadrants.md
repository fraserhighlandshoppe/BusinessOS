# Cardone Ventures Business Quadrants

## The Four Quadrants
1. **Marketing**
2. **People**
3. **Operations**
4. **Finances**

**Truth**: Cash follows Attention. Attention follows Money.

## KPI Metrics
### Good KPIs
- How many clients has staff brought in?
- How many clients has existing clients brought in?
- ROI
- Cost per customer acquisition
- Customer Lifetime value

### Vanity KPIs
- Likes
- Comments

**Insight**: Engagement doesn't necessarily convert.

**Rule**: Revenue is Vanity. Profit is Sanity!

## Best CRM
**The best CRM is the one you use.**

CRM helps improve customer experience.
CRM is more business intelligence tool than marketing tool.

**Track lead sources.**

## The Real Problem
Everyone says it's Marketing, then Ops, then People, then Financing.

**Reality**: It's really a Person Problem.

## Customer Understanding
**Question**: What are top 3 attributes of your ideal customer?

**If pause**: You don't know.
**If don't know**: How can you attract them?
**If can't attract**: How can you sell them?

**Biggest Problem**: If your people don't know who/what you're doing, that's the biggest problem.

## Growing the Business
People take foot off accelerator when growing.
They worry about what might happen, distracting from good happening.

## Boredom & Breakthrough
Most people get bored before breakthrough.
Those who make it through re-discover fun through delegation.

## Customer Segmentation
Take top 30% of clients. What do they look like?
Then go find more of them.
Trim off bottom 30% to make room for new.

## Scaling Your Business
- Who are you comparing yourself against?
- Set your goals
- Enlarge your circle (hang with people who achieved what you want)
- Promote with energy into action
- Don't get caught up in technology for indirect contact

**Employee Marketing Test**: If marketing not working and >5 employees, ask 3 questions each. 100% chance no common answers.

**Hiring Standard**: Don't hire "good". Hire remarkable.
- remarkable > excellent > great > good > average > poor

**Words Matter**: The words you use extend yourself. Use right words so employees properly promote you.
