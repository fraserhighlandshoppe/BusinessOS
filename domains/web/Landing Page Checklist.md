# The 16-Point Landing Page Checklist

Landing Pages are fundamental to digital marketing.

Each time you build one go through this 16-point checklist to give your landing page the best chance to succeed.

## 1. Market Callout
Your visitor needs to know they're in the right place, so make sure you call out to them either directly (i.e. Attention: ______) or indirectly with recognizable images and vocabulary.

## 2. Mobile First
Mobile visitors need the same kind of guidance throughout the page, be able to access the same resources, and have the same compulsion to complete the action as their desktop counterparts.

Designing for mobile devices is not simply about moving blocks around to fit a smaller screen, but must focus on a deep awareness of the behaviour differences on mobile as opposed to desktop devices.

## 3. Load Speed
The longer a page takes to load the lower the conversion. TEST!

## 4. Clear and Concise
The best landing pages have a single message and make a single offer. Make sure your landing page isn't trying to do too much.

## 5. Easily Understood
If a visitor can't figure out what you're offering in 5 seconds or less, you'll lose them. Perform the 5-second test with friends or colleagues and make sure your landing page passes.

Focus on using language which builds trust between the brand, product or service and the visitors.

## 6. Compelling Headline
You need a clear, concise, benefit-rich headline that grabs your reader's attention and tells them they've come to the right place. Count the number of benefits in the headline.

## 7. CTA Above the Fold
Most visitors won't scroll below the fold, so if you're making a free offer, give them a chance to take action without scrolling.

## 8. Contrasting Button Color
The button color should contrast (NOT blend in) with the surrounding design elements.

## 9. Custom Button Text
"Submit" is not good enough. Test button text that gives a specific command or speaks to the end result (i.e. "Free Instant Access").

## 10. Social Proof
Social share icons, "As seen on" logos, testimonials, or referencing the number of downloads/subscribers all let visitors know they're making a smart decision.

## 11. Limited Navigation
The landing page should not distract the visitor with the ability to navigate away from the page. Only one other link at most.

## 12. Uses Visual Cues
Incorporate arrows, boxes and other visual devices to draw the eye to the call-to-action area.

## 13. Hero Shot
Typically an image or graphical representation of the lead magnet will bump conversions, but not always. Test with and without.

## 14. Limited Form Fields
Don't ask for information you don't need! If you only plan to followup via email, just ask for name and email, at most.

## 15. Source Congruency
The text and imagery on the landing page should match (ideally exactly) the text and imagery in the ad or creative that brought the visitor to the page.

## 16. Brand Consistency
The overall look and feel should be consistent with your core brand.

## 17. Enable Sharing
Make it easy and obvious for visitors to share on Facebook and Twitter.

## 18. Visible Privacy Policy and TOS
Privacy policies and terms of service are required for some advertising sites and help with conversions.
