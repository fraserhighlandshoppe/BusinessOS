# Cold Email Marketing Tips

Source: Critiques by Sean McPheat

## Email Mistakes to Avoid

**Received email example**:
```
Subject: Special Offer

Good Afternoon,

Sorry for the unsolicited email. I'm under the impression that you're 
responsible for making recruitment decisions at MTD but if you're not 
could you please pass this onto the person who is responsible?

We specialise in recruiting top sales people and we've currently got a 
special offer with a flat fee of just £2,000 per position.

I have attached our company brochure for background on what makes XYZ 
Recruitment unique and I look forward to hearing from you.

Best Regards
John Smith
```

## 8 Critical Mistakes

### 1. Spammy Subject Line
**Bad**: "Special Offer"
**Good**: 
- Question: "Quick Question About XYZ Company"
- Idea: "Idea to help you prove the ROI of your projects"

### 2. Impersonal Greeting
**Bad**: "Good Afternoon"
**Good**: Use personalized variables like [[FIRSTNAME]]

### 3. Apologizing
**Bad**: "Sorry for the unsolicited email"
**Good**: Never apologize - shows confidence

### 4. No Homework
**Bad**: "I'm under the impression you're responsible..."
**Good**: Research the recipient thoroughly

### 5. Too Vague - So What?
**Bad**: "We specialize in recruiting top sales people"
**Good**: Show proof of results, build value first

### 6. Making Them Work
**Bad**: "I've attached our company brochure"
**Good**: Make it easy - provide key info in email body

### 7. Generic Content
**Bad**: Standard templates
**Good**: Customized for each recipient

### 8. Poor Call to Action
**Bad**: Vague requests
**Good**: Crystal clear next step

## Best Practices

### Subject Lines
- Focus on questions OR benefits/ideas
- Create curiosity
- Assume you've done your homework

### Writing Style
- Never apologize
- Address actual person by name
- Be specific about their needs
- Show proof of value

### Structure
- Single, clear message
- Make it scannable
- Provide value before asking
- Clear, simple CTA
