# The Simple Email Trick That Makes Following Up Effective

Source: [Entrepreneur Article](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/246285)
Author: Issamar Ginzberg
Date: April 30, 2015

## The Follow-Up Dilemma

**Problem**: Should I follow up with a prospect who hasn't responded?
**Challenge**: Stay "top of mind" without appearing harassing or pushy.

## The Elegant Solution

This technique can **double your chances** of moving relationships forward.

### The Method
1. Research your client's industry on Google News
2. Find relevant news they would find valuable
3. Email them the link (don't mention previous email)

**That's it.** No follow-up mention. Just provide value.

### Why It Works
- Shows you've been thinking of them
- Gets your name on their radar
- Often gets a "thank you" response
- When they thank you, they mention your previous email

### Example News to Share
- Pending legislation affecting their business
- An entrepreneur changing industry models
- Local economic news

## Using on HARO

For media pitches (Help A Reporter Out):
1. Email pitch with "meat" - not just contact info
2. Research topic-relevant information
3. Email additional relevant article to journalist
4. Don't pitch again or follow up

**Result**: Only respondent with two shots at mention. Provides tremendous value to reporter.

## The Boomerang Effect

By spending minutes showing you're willing to be of value, the boomerang effect kicks in:
- They reciprocate with mentions or responses
- Opens doors to further interaction
- Builds relationship naturally

## Key Principles
- Be relevant
- Pitch reporters thoroughly
- Do background research
- Develop media contacts
