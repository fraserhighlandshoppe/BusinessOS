# Emotional Branding for FHS

## Overview
Focus on emotional fulfillment and lasting relationships with customers in the Highland arts community.

## Core Principles

### Engagement → Emotions → Experience
Customer journey starts with engagement, builds emotions, creates experience.

### People-Based Economy
- Customers have power
- Focus on individual needs
- Build deep, lasting relationships

## Emotional Branding Framework

### Before Purchase Decision

#### Buying for Need (Rational)
- Price
- Practicality
- Rational factors
- Convenience

#### Buying for Desire (Emotional)
- Emotional connection
- No price tag - evaluate value
- Self-interest fulfillment

### Building Emotional Connection

**Mirror Exercise**: Look in the mirror and ask:
- Who are our core clients?
- What do we sell best?
- What promises can we make?

**Relationship Building**:
- Understand customer preferences
- Earn trust
- Stand by promises

**Crisis Management**:
- Handle problems gracefully
- Even uncontrollable issues (weather) need attention
- Make customers feel important

## Brand Identity vs Personality

### Identity
- Descriptive - what you are
- Recognition-based
- "Being known" doesn't mean "being loved"

### Personality
- Character and charisma
- Shows uniqueness
- Why you're special
- "Being loved" requires personality

## Storytelling for FHS

### Emotional Trigger Words
- Delightful
- Exotic
- Magical
- Energized
- Easy
- Blissful

### Story Structure
1. **Setting** - Where/When
2. **Action** - What happens
3. **Result** - Outcome/benefit

### Story Types (Have 3 Ready)
- Customer success stories
- Product transformation stories
- Community impact stories

### Learning Customer Stories
- Be a good listener
- Reflect back what you heard
- Get to emotional root: "Why do they want this?"

### Visualization Techniques
- Ask customers to close eyes
- Guide them to "vacation sweat spot"
- Use all 5 senses:
  - Feeling
  - Seeing
  - Hearing
  - Tasting
  - Smelling

## Communication Channels

### One-to-One
- At the desk
- Phone calls
- Email

### One-to-Many
- Newsletters
- Social media
- Public presentations

## Dos and Don'ts

### Do
- Focus on individual needs
- Be genuine and caring
- Don't fake it
- Be open and connective
- Improve shopping experience
- Follow up before and after purchase

### Don't
- Worry about immediate sale
- Focus on vanity/generic marketing
- Use transformational language
- Replace "vendor" with "partner"

## Language Upgrades

| Old Term | New Term |
|---|---|
| Booking | Create/Design/Build/Craft |
| Sell | Work With |
| Agent | Advisor/Expert |
| Vendor | Partner |

## Key Insights

- Facts tell, stories sell
- Information leads to analysis
- Emotion leads to action
- Quality for right price is expected
- Preference makes the sale
- Intimate marketing and engagement required
- Vanilla/generic marketing fails - needs targeting
