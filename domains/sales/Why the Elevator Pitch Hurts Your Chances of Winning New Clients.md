# Why the Elevator Pitch Hurts Your Chances of Winning New Clients

Source: [Entrepreneur Article](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/246285)
Author: Issamar Ginzberg
Date: May 18, 2015

## The Problem with Elevator Pitches

### The "Deer in Headlights" Syndrome
When asked "What do you do?", entrepreneurs often freeze and deliver a rehearsed elevator pitch that puts them in the prospect's mental filing cabinet alongside dozens of other mortgage brokers, insurance agents, etc.

### The Filing Cabinet Mentality
As soon as you say what you do, you're categorized and mentally filed away. Prospects lose interest and move on.

"I'm a mortgage broker... working for [name]. We do mortgages in the states of..."

Result: Eyes glaze over, prospect fights the urge to yawn.

## The Better Approach

**Goal**: Get an opportunity for a real conversation, not a sound bite.

### The Pattern
When someone asks "What do you do?":

**Them**: "Hello! Nice to meet you! What do you do?"

**You**: "What I do doesn't fit into one of those categories that people have in their mental filing cabinet, so it's kind of hard to explain in a classification. You know those people who have businesses and want to make much more money by doing what they already do?"

**Them**: "Yes..."

**You**: "Well, that's what I do..."

**Them**: "How?"

## Why This Works

1. **Not Easily Classifiable**: They can't simply file you away
2. **Creates Curiosity**: They have to engage to understand
3. **Opens Conversation**: No time constraint, natural dialogue
4. **Educational**: Shows value immediately
5. **Entertainment**: Engaging delivery

## Key Takeaway

The elevator pitch should not tell them what you do. It should get an opening for curiosity that leads to a real conversation where you can deliver genuine value.
