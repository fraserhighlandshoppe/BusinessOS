# Grant Cardone Objection Handling

## Reasons People Object

### Common Objections
- "I need to think about it."
- "Let me talk to my spouse about it."
- "I don't have the money"
- "Time"

### Root Cause
They lacked **trust**:
- Trust in you
- Trust in product/service
- Trust lost during sales process

**When they don't trust, they don't buy.**

## GC Objective Handling

### Preparation
- Practice during training time
- **DON'T practice during the close**
- Shouldn't be surprised by objection
- Objections you walk in prepared to handle

### The 2 Real Objections
1. **They aren't the decision maker**
2. **They don't think it will work**

## Handling the Objections

### Technique: Agree, Then Move
1. **Agree with them**
2. **Move into negotiation/close**
3. Agreement = no friction (doesn't mean they're right)

### Example: "Your price is too high"
> "You're right, and I get it. In fact, I've heard the same thing from a lot of my customers. Now let me show you why and why this deal is a no-brainer."

### Example: "I can't make such a rash decision"
> "I agree. In fact, I never make rash decisions, and I wouldn't expect you to, either. In fact, let me show you my competitor's offers so you can see why this would be a solid decision for you right now."

## Tanner Chidester Price Handling

1. **Close** (present price)
2. **"Too much"**
3. **Agree**
4. "Is there anything other than the price stopping you?" (**Tie down** - if not, they'll change objection later)
5. "If we can figure out price are you willing to move forward?"
   - **Don't throw out decreasing amounts** - sounds desperate
6. "How much cash on hand do you have?" (Savings, CC, Crypto, etc)
   - **You must get a number**
   - "I can't help you if you don't give me a number"
7. "I Have XXX"
   - **Cool, let's put that down and work out payment program**
8. "I have X"
   - Have a floor you aren't going below
   - "Hey, the lowest I can go is XX. Can you make that work?"
   - No? "Ok, when you're in a better financial position reach out."
   - If it was your friend: "OK, you can't afford it today. Let's save up..."
