# Finding Employees Eager to Both Work and Grow

## Source
- [Entrepreneur Article](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/246285)
- Author: Issamar Ginzberg
- Date: February 17, 2015

## The Excel Mistake

**Story**: Hired a personal assistant who did well at tasks but failed on a critical Excel job due to not understanding 16-digit number limitations.

**Result**: Had to stay up all night fixing the data.

**Lesson**: There are two types of workers:
1. Those who "do the minimum" - follow instructions exactly
2. Those who "work and grow" - try to understand WHY and find better solutions

## The Hiring Problem

Traditional resume matching is futile because:
- Resumes mirror job descriptions exactly
- Doesn't reveal problem-solving ability
- Misses growth potential

## Better Hiring Questions

### Knowledge-Based Questions
- How many pages are in the Babylonian Talmud? (tests ability to grasp unfamiliar material)
- What airport is YMQ? Why is it named that?

### Skill-Based Questions
- What are three alternatives to [invoicing software] and how do they compare?
- How much will a car rental cost in NYC, pickup/drop-off JFK, week of March 8th?

For the car rental question, look for:
- Checking pick-up availability at unusual hours
- Finding coupon codes
- Calling ahead when needed

## The Excel Test

**New Hiring Question**: "Please take this list of 16-digit numbers and put them in an Excel spreadsheet..."

**What to Look For**:
- Takes time to understand the problem
- Has the skill to deliver correctly
- Shows initiative to find solutions

## Key Insight

Many unemployed people aren't thinking about "making the employer happy" - they just follow instructions. Smart hiring is an investment in both your future and your employee's future.

## Hiring Philosophy

Don't just hire for current skills - hire for growth potential. The ability to "think on your feet" and deliver better results is what separates those who merely work from those who work and grow.

**Google's Approach**: Uses mathematical challenges on billboards to recruit - if you don't get the math, you don't find the hiring page.
