# Staying Motivated - Grant Cardone

## Challenge Yourself First
Most people do things that don't challenge them.

**Do things that challenge you most.**
- May cause anxiety or fear
- Underneath = motivation

**Example**: Make most difficult sales calls first.
- Win = fired up
- Lose = at least it's out of the way

**Most people take easiest first** - not challenging = not motivated.

### Practical Application
- List 5 things that challenge you most
- Do at least one today
- Notice how you feel afterward

## Daily Targets

### Why Focus on Daily Target?
- See accomplishments = fuel
- Makes you feel good about yourself
- Need lifetime, yearly, monthly, AND daily targets

### Daily Target Questions
- What do you want to accomplish today?
- Who do you want to contact?
- What would make today great?
- Did I wake up on time?
- Did I get workout in?
- Did I spend time with family?

### Practical Application
Write down daily target. Document what happens when focused.

## Ask Mentors for Guidance

### Who to Ask
- Someone with serious success
- Multiple entrepreneurial efforts
- Success in every area of life

### Approach
- Reach out for guidance
- Ask for help, direction
- "I don't want anything except guidance once/twice monthly"

**Realize their time is precious.**
- They want to help
- This inspires them to say yes
- Listen more than talk
- Take, use, execute

### Practical Application
Identify 2 people more successful than you. Arrange to meet/speak for guidance.

## Motivating Your Team

### Focus on Goals
Goals are most important for motivation.
- Daily and throughout day (not once/yearly)
- Celebrate meeting goals
- Analyze failures
- Set based on team and individual performance

### Set Achievable Goals
Avoid overwhelm, especially holidays.
Set realistic goals within reasonable timeframe.

### Plan Post-Holiday Celebration
Celebrate hard work with team party.

### Address Problems Ahead
If issues (tight deadline, workload), ask for help early.

### Give Back
Opportunity to give time/skills to worthy cause = motivation.

### Give Team Autonomy
Let each decide how to meet goals and close sales.
- More likely to try harder
- Less likely to give up when tough

### Allow Creativity
- Let team try different closing methods
- Experiment with delivery (video, audio)
- Track what works
- Don't be too detailed - kills creativity

### Reward Good Performance
Positive consequences increase behavior much more than avoiding punishment.
