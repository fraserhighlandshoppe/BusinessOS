# 7 Habits of Highly Effective Traders

By Jared P Erni

## Habit 1: Be Proactive
**Why**: Most traders lose money because they are reactive, not proactive.

**Being Reactive**: Blame other people or circumstances for problems.

**Being Proactive**: Accept 100% responsibility for all actions. When accepting responsibility:
- Close door to excuses
- Open door to learning
- Learn from mistakes (don't repeat them)

**Result**: Empowered to learn, progressively better trader.

## Habit 2: Begin with an End in Mind
**Meaning**: Plan the trade first, then trade the plan.

**Why**: Without planning, no long-term success.
- Replace emotional worry with systematic actions
- Know entry, add, stop-loss, trailing stop, exit strategy before entering

**Key Insight**: Markets will "throw you off track" - only disciplined traders benefit.

**Analogy**: Planning a trade = planning for unexpected on a trip.

## Habit 3: First Thing First
**Missing Step**: Find a system that works for YOU.

**Questions to Ask**:
- What kind of personality am I?
- What trading suits my personality?
- Am I comfortable with slow, steady decisions or fast-paced stress?
- Do I like action and quick decisions?
- Full-time, part-time, or monthly trading?
- What annual rate of return do I want?
- Which top traders do I admire and why?

**Result**: Comfortable system = confidence + enjoyment = success.

## Habit 4: Think Win-Win
**Concept**: When professionals win, you win too.

**Reality**: You are a small player scooping scraps.
- Understand big players move markets
- Recognize patterns they create
- "Think win-win" - when they win, you win

## Habit 5: First Seek to Understand
**Key Question**: Are you genuinely interested in trading or just lured by money?

**Why**: Years of trial/error, effort until successful.
- First couple years = university
- Market is teacher, account is tuition

**Mindset**: Stick it out, don't give up. Rewards come.

## Habit 6: Synergize
**Principle**: Network with people who are successful.

**Benefits**:
- Support and learning from experiences
- Share goals, knowledge, experiences
- Speed up learning curve
- "Together Everyone Achieves More" (TEAM)

**Action**: Join trading clubs, form groups with like-minded people.

## Habit 7: Sharpen the Saw
**Reality**: Trading is extremely stressful.

**Balance Required**:
- Don't spend all time studying
- Be reasonable with study time
- Work hard and play hard

**Warning**: Don't depend on trading for major excitement.

**Benefit**: Breaks help you return with better insight, motivation, fresh look.

## Trading Analogy
Market = big apple tree surrounded by alligators.
Bridge = path to tree (rickety, few make it).
Only those with "guts and right steps" succeed.

Apply these habits and you'll cross the bridge to real trading success.
