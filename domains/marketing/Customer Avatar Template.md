# Customer Avatar Template

Source: Digital Marketer

## Why Use a Customer Avatar?

Setting up your customer avatar may seem broad or based on assumptions. But you need it all on one page to see and test assumptions.

**Use data and validatable assumptions** to complete the worksheet. If based solely on gut, targeting will miss - costing time and money.

Fill out with qualities of your **best customers** (people you want to sell to) because the avatar can be a self-fulfilling prophecy.

**Key**: Describe people you want to ATTRACT, not just people you've sold to.

## Using the Avatar

Write narrative from avatar perspective to describe:
- Current experience
- How they discover your solution
- Their transformation
- How it meets unmet needs
- Create "ultimate scenario"

**Script out dialog** with Customer Avatar from first meeting to purchase.

## Customer Avatar Structure

### Who (Segment Identity)
Each avatar represents distinct customer segment.

**Persona Name**: Identifies segment (e.g., "Freelancer Fran", "Agency Al")
**Photo**: Add representative photo to make avatar real

### Background
- Level of education
- Job (occupation, job title, income)
- Career
- Family (marital status, kids - number, gender, age)

### Demographics
Brings avatar to life - easier to write ads to.
- Gender
- Age
- Location
- Income (personal and household)
- Values (Eco/Green? Fair trade?)
- Beliefs
- Lifestyle
- Influencers:
  - What they watch
  - What they read (Books, Magazines, Blogs, Websites)
  - What they listen to
  - What they attend (Conferences) - WHY?

### Gather Customer Source of Information
- Talk to people in avatar group
- Pull from personal experience
- Google Search (Top books, Best conferences, Top blogs, Best groups)
- **"But no one else would"** - Get niche enough to exclude non-customers

### Identifiers
- Demeanor
- Communication preference
- Jargon, lingo, slang
- Themes, stories, phrases used to describe challenges and solutions
