# 7 Tips to Make Sure Your LinkedIn Picture Is Helping, Not Hurting Your Prospects

## Source
Harrison Monarth, January 13, 2015

## Why It Matters
Poor profile pictures can damage your professional brand and career opportunities.

**91% of recruiters use social media to screen candidates.**

## 7 Tips

### 1. Appear Approachable
- Professional headshot
- Show spark - professional but someone people want to work with
- Average, typical faces are most trustworthy

### 2. Remember: This is About YOU
- Front and center
- Focus on you, not distracting background
- Close-ups work best - head and shoulders fill frame

### 3. Keep It Fresh
- If old enough to buy alcohol, get new one
- Don't want different expectation when meeting face-to-face

### 4. Look the Part
- Clothes matter - dress for job you want
- For C-suite: Look healthy is more important than looking smart
- Schedule haircut/spa treatment before shoot

### 5. Skip Facebook-esque Photos
- No party shots, vacation pics, memes, cartoons
- No pictures of partners, kids, pets on LinkedIn

### 6. No Selfies Allowed
**Three problems with selfies**:
1. Stigma - not acceptable for professional endeavors
2. Lower quality - limited control for light, etc.
3. Lazy - hand camera to someone else

### 7. What Works for Brad Pitt
- Moderate Photoshop allowed
- Slight adjustments to tone, lighting, blemish removal
- Sharp contrast can be softened
- Subtle Photoshopping = polished appearance

## 8 Types of Photos to Never Use
(From Kim Lachance Shandrow)

LinkedIn can ban you for 3 strikes of bad photos.

**Never use**:
1. Not actually a photo of you
2. Not a headshot
3. Party shots
4. Selfies
5. Vacation photos
6. Memes or cartoons
7. Pictures of family/pets
8. Inappropriate content

**LinkedIn is for professional networking** - not Man Crush Monday, not swiping, not stalking high school crush.
